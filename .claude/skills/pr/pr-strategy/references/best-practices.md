## 1. PR Strategy Frameworks

### 1.1 The PESO Model

The PESO Model (created by Gini Dietrich of Spin Sucks) is the dominant PR strategy framework in 2026. It integrates four media types into a cohesive communications strategy:

| Media Type | Definition | Key Function | Control Level |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Paid** | Visibility you buy — ads, sponsored content, influencer partnerships | Reach new audiences quickly, amplify owned content | High |
| **Earned** | Coverage you earn — press mentions, reviews, UGC, journalist features | Build credibility and trust through third-party validation | Low |
| **Shared** | Social engagement — posts, comments, shares, community interaction | Build community, enable real-time audience dialogue | Medium |
| **Owned** | Assets you control — website, blog, newsletter, newsroom | Control narrative, deliver value directly, build authority | Full |

**The 2026 principle:** The smartest brands build outward from owned media. Your newsroom is the only place you can fully manage context, accuracy, and reuse. Earned, shared, and paid amplify what owned creates.

**Source:** Improvado, "PESO Framework: Complete Guide for Digital Marketers 2026"; Brandpoint, "PESO Model for PR"

### 1.2 The Integrated Approach

Modern PR does not choose between earned and paid — it integrates both. The PESO model ensures:
- Paid amplifies the best owned content
- Earned validates the brand narrative to skeptical audiences
- Shared keeps the community engaged between major PR moments
- Owned serves as the canonical source for all messaging

Each media type reinforces the others, creating a funnel from awareness through conversion.

### 1.3 PR Strategy Planning Process

1. **Audit current state** — Where does the brand appear today? What is the current media mix?
2. **Define objectives** — What does PR need to achieve? (Awareness, credibility, lead gen, crisis repair)
3. **Identify target audiences** — Who needs to hear the message? (Media, customers, partners, investors)
4. **Develop narrative pillars** — 2-3 core stories the brand wants to be known for
5. **Select channels and tactics** — Map PESO channels to each objective
6. **Build media targets** — Tiered list of outlets, journalists, and influencers
7. **Create calendar** — Align PR moments with business milestones
8. **Measure and iterate** — Track coverage, sentiment, and business impact

## 2. Media Tiers

### 2.1 Tier Definitions

| Tier | Type | Examples | Impact | Difficulty |
|------|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Tier 1** | Major national/international outlets | NYT, WSJ, Forbes, TechCrunch, BBC, CNN | Massive reach, authority, SEO value | Very high — requires strong angles and relationships |
| **Tier 2** | Trade and industry publications | Industry-specific magazines, trade journals, SaaS blogs | Targeted reach, high credibility with decision-makers | Medium — requires niche expertise and relevance |
| **Tier 3** | Niche outlets, blogs, podcasts | Indie podcasts, personal blogs, niche newsletters, local media | Authentic reach, engaged audiences, long-tail SEO | Lower — more accessible, relationship-driven |

### 2.2 Tier Strategy

**Tier 1 — Authority Play:**
- Reserve for major announcements, launches, and thought leadership
- Requires differentiated story angles with broad appeal
- Long lead times (pitch 4-8 weeks in advance for features)
- Relationship-intensive — invest in journalist relationships before you need coverage
- One Tier 1 placement can unlock Tier 2 and 3 coverage by association

**Tier 2 — Credibility Play:**
- Ideal for establishing industry expertise and thought leadership
- Bylined articles, expert commentary, panel participation
- Shorter lead times (2-4 weeks)
- Often leads to speaking engagements and partnership opportunities
- Trade press coverage influences buying decisions in B2B

**Tier 3 — Frequency and Reach Play:**
- Build volume and SEO backlink profile
- Podcast appearances, guest posts, newsletter features
- Fastest to secure (1-2 week turnaround)
- Often the most engaged audiences (niche communities trust their outlets)
- Compound effect — 10 Tier 3 placements can match the reach of 1 Tier 1

### 2.3 Media Mix Allocation

A balanced PR strategy distributes effort across all tiers:

| Tier | Effort Allocation | Expected Volume |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Tier 1 | 30% of PR effort | 1-3 placements per quarter |
| Tier 2 | 40% of PR effort | 3-8 placements per quarter |
| Tier 3 | 30% of PR effort | 8-20+ placements per quarter |

**Source:** AMW, "7 PR Trends That Will Define 2026"; Presspage, "Paid vs Earned vs Owned Media"

## 3. Narrative Building

### 3.1 The Narrative Framework

Every PR strategy needs 2-3 core narrative pillars that all communications ladder up to:

**Pillar Structure:**
```
MASTER NARRATIVE
"[Brand] is [position] because [evidence]"

Pillar 1: [Data / Innovation Story]
- Key message
- Supporting proof points
- Target outlets / angles

Pillar 2: [Customer Impact Story]
- Key message
- Supporting proof points (case studies, testimonials)
- Target outlets / angles

Pillar 3: [Point of View / Thought Leadership]
- Key message
- Contrarian or differentiated perspective
- Target outlets / angles
```

### 3.2 Narrative Building Techniques

**The "Only" Test:**
Can you fill in: "We are the only [category] that [unique attribute]"? If not, the narrative is not differentiated enough.

**The "So What" Ladder:**
- Feature: "We launched X"
- Benefit: "Which means customers can Y"
- Impact: "Which results in Z improvement"
- Narrative: "This is part of our mission to [bigger story]"

Always go at least 3 levels deep. Journalists care about impact, not features.

**The "Trend + You" Formula:**
- Industry trend: "[Trend] is reshaping [industry]"
- Brand connection: "[Brand] has been [doing/pioneering/proving] this for [time]"
- Proof: "Here's the evidence: [data, case study, customer quote]"
- Forward look: "Here's what comes next: [prediction with credibility]"

### 3.3 Story Angle Development

Strong PR angles share these characteristics:
- **Timely** — Connected to current news, trends, or seasonal moments
- **Novel** — New data, contrarian take, or first-of-its-kind
- **Impactful** — Real consequences for real people
- **Credible** — Backed by evidence, data, or expert authority
- **Visual** — Can be illustrated with compelling imagery or data visualization

Weak angles that journalists ignore:
- "Company announces product" (unless it is genuinely newsworthy)
- Self-congratulatory content without external validation
- Generic industry commentary without a unique perspective
- Press releases disguised as news

## 4. Owned vs Earned vs Paid Media Strategy

### 4.1 Owned Media — The Foundation

Owned media is the starting point for all PR in 2026:

**Core owned assets:**
- Brand newsroom / blog
- Email newsletter
- Podcast (if applicable)
- Social media profiles (overlap with Shared)
- Case studies and white papers
- Video content library

**Why owned leads:**
- Full control over context, accuracy, and messaging
- Permanent home for all brand content (earned placements can disappear)
- SEO value compounds over time
- Provides source material for earned and paid amplification
- No dependency on third-party editorial decisions

### 4.2 Earned Media — The Credibility Multiplier

**Tactics to earn coverage:**
- Build genuine journalist relationships (engage with their work, provide value)
- Provide exclusive data, research, or insights
- Make spokespeople available for expert commentary
- Create newsworthy moments (product launches, milestones, events)
- Monitor for reactive opportunities (newsjacking relevant stories)
- Encourage customer reviews and testimonials on third-party platforms
- Leverage user-generated content for amplification

**Earned media value proposition:**
- Third-party validation that money cannot buy
- Trust signal — audiences trust earned coverage more than ads
- SEO backlink value from authoritative publications
- Social proof that compounds over time

### 4.3 Paid Media — The Amplifier

**PR-specific paid tactics:**
- Sponsored content / native advertising in target publications
- Social media promotion of owned content and earned coverage
- Influencer partnerships for product reviews and mentions
- Paid distribution of press releases (when appropriate)
- Targeted ads reaching specific journalist or analyst audiences

**Budget allocation principle:**
- Use paid to amplify your best owned content to new audiences
- Use paid to boost high-performing earned coverage
- Never use paid as a substitute for genuine earned media
- Test with small budgets, scale what works

### 4.4 The Integration Flow

```
Owned Content → Pitched for Earned Coverage → Earned Placements Amplified via Paid → Shared Across Social → Drives Back to Owned Properties
```

Each PESO element feeds the next, creating a virtuous cycle.

**Source:** Cision, "The Essential Guide to Owned, Paid, and Earned Media"; Improvado, "PESO Framework" (2026); ILearnLot, "Earned Media Strategy: Success 2026"

## 5. PR Measurement

### 5.1 Key PR Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Media placements | Volume of coverage across tiers | Visibility and reach |
| Share of voice | Brand mentions vs. competitors | Market positioning |
| Sentiment score | Positive/negative/neutral ratio | Brand perception |
| Domain authority of placements | Quality of linking publications | SEO and credibility impact |
| Referral traffic from coverage | Clicks from earned placements | Business impact |
| Message pull-through | Key messages appearing in coverage | Narrative effectiveness |
| Social amplification | Shares and engagement on coverage | Content resonance |
| Lead / pipeline attribution | Business outcomes from PR activity | ROI justification |

### 5.2 Measurement Cadence

| Cadence | What to Report |
|---------|---------------|
| Weekly | New placements, mention volume, sentiment snapshot |
| Monthly | Coverage analysis, share of voice, message pull-through |
| Quarterly | Strategic review, tier performance, narrative effectiveness, competitive positioning |

## 6. PR Strategy Checklist

Before finalizing any PR strategy, verify:

- [ ] 2-3 narrative pillars defined with supporting proof points
- [ ] Media tier targets identified (specific outlets and journalists per tier)
- [ ] PESO channels mapped to each strategic objective
- [ ] PR calendar aligned with business milestones and quarterly roadmap
- [ ] Spokesperson preparation complete (key messages, Q&A, media training)
- [ ] Measurement framework established with clear KPIs
- [ ] Crisis communication protocols defined
- [ ] Owned media assets ready to serve as the canonical source
- [ ] Story angles developed and validated against the "So What" test

## 7. 2026 PR Trends

1. **AI in PR:** AI tools for media monitoring, sentiment analysis, and pitch personalization are standard. Human judgment remains essential for strategy, relationships, and crisis management.
2. **Creator/influencer overlap:** PR and influencer marketing are converging. Micro-influencers increasingly replace traditional media for certain audience segments.
3. **Data-driven storytelling:** Proprietary data and original research are the strongest pitch assets.
4. **Authenticity premium:** Audiences detect and reject overly polished, inauthentic messaging. Raw, transparent communication outperforms corporate speak.
5. **Owned-first strategy:** Brands that invest in owned media (newsroom, newsletter, podcast) have more leverage in earned media conversations.

**Source:** AMW, "7 PR Trends That Will Define 2026"; The 2026 Media Mix, EngageAdCom
