# Opportunity Brief — Best Practices Reference

This reference consolidates opportunity assessment methodology from multiple frameworks.
Use it as the methodological backbone when building opportunity briefs.

---

## 1. Brief Structure Standards

A strong opportunity brief follows a consistent structure that moves from context to recommendation. The sections below synthesize the Neal Cabage Opportunity Brief Template, the Productfolio template, and the Niran Kundapur product opportunity brief format.

### 1.1 Recommended Sections

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Executive Summary** | Top 3-5 things the reader must know. Key points pulled from every section below. Write this last. |
| 2 | **Opportunity Statement** | What trend, event, or shift creates an opening? State the opportunity, not the solution. |
| 3 | **Problem / Gap** | What problem does this opportunity address? Who feels the pain? What is currently missing from the conversation? |
| 4 | **Target Audience** | Who are you reaching? Map to existing audience personas. Note any new segments this could unlock. |
| 5 | **Landscape & Competition** | Who else is already talking about this? What angles are covered? What is NOT being said? |
| 6 | **Unique Angle & Differentiation** | What can the user say that no one else is saying? How does this connect to Brand DNA? |
| 7 | **Timeliness Assessment** | How urgent is this? What is the action window? (See Section 2 below for full framework.) |
| 8 | **Opportunity Score** | Quantified evaluation using the scoring matrix. (See Section 3 below.) |
| 9 | **Recommended Action** | Go / no-go / wait recommendation with rationale. |
| 10 | **Content Formats & Channels** | Which formats and platforms fit this opportunity best? |
| 11 | **Success Metrics** | How will we know this worked? Define leading and lagging indicators. |
| 12 | **Timeline & Next Steps** | Specific action items, owners, and deadlines. |

### 1.2 Brief Formatting Rules

- **Length:** First draft can be 1-2 pages with bullet points. Final brief should be 4-6 pages max.
- **Header:** Mark early versions as "Working Draft for Discussion."
- **Tone:** Direct, evidence-based, actionable. No fluff.
- **Recommendation clarity:** The go/no-go recommendation must be unambiguous and supported by the timeliness assessment and opportunity score.

### 1.3 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Writing a solution before defining the opportunity.
- Skipping the competitive landscape — if you don't know what's already been said, you risk repeating it.
- Conflating "interesting" with "actionable" — a trend can be real but wrong for this brand.
- Ignoring timing — a great opportunity with a missed window is worthless.
- Burying the recommendation — put the go/no-go decision front and center.

## 2. Timeliness Assessment Framework

Timeliness determines whether an opportunity is actionable *right now* or merely interesting. This framework draws from the ITONICS trend evaluation methodology (velocity, speed of change, time-to-market impact) and the POEM Framework's timing force.

### 2.1 The Timing Window Model

Every opportunity has a timing window — the period during which acting on it delivers maximum impact. The window has four phases:

| Phase | Description | Action Posture |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **1. Emerging** | Early signals only. Few people are talking about it. Limited mainstream awareness. | Monitor. Prepare assets but don't publish yet. Low risk of being late, high risk of being too early. |
| **2. Rising** | Conversation accelerating. Media coverage increasing. Early movers are establishing positions. | **Act now.** This is the sweet spot for thought leadership — enough awareness to be relevant, few enough voices to stand out. |
| **3. Peak** | Everyone is talking about it. High saturation. Hard to differentiate. | Act only if you have a genuinely contrarian or unique angle. Otherwise, the window has passed for thought leadership. |
| **4. Declining** | Conversation fading. Audience fatigue. New trends replacing it. | Skip unless doing a retrospective or "lessons learned" piece. |

### 2.2 Timeliness Scoring Criteria

Rate each factor on a 1-5 scale:

| Factor | 1 (Low) | 3 (Medium) | 5 (High) |
|--------|---------|------------|----------|
| **Velocity of conversation** | Slow, niche discussion | Growing steadily | Rapid acceleration, trending |
| **Current window phase** | Declining or too early | Emerging | Rising (optimal) |
| **Shelf life of content** | Days (news cycle) | Weeks | Months (evergreen angle possible) |
| **First-mover advantage** | Many voices already established | Some competition but room to differentiate | Wide open — few quality takes exist |
| **Audience readiness** | Audience unaware or fatigued | Audience curious | Audience actively seeking perspectives |

**Timeliness Score:** Sum of all five factors (out of 25).

| Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|---------------|
| 20-25 | **Urgent — act within 48 hours.** Peak timing window. Prioritize this. |
| 14-19 | **Timely — act within 1-2 weeks.** Good window with some flexibility. |
| 8-13 | **Monitor — not urgent.** Set a reminder to reassess in 1-2 weeks. |
| 5-7 | **Pass or archive.** Window closed or not yet open. Revisit only if conditions change. |

### 2.3 Speed of Change Indicators

Borrowed from the ITONICS innovation methodology, track these signals to assess velocity:

- **Search volume trends:** Is search interest rising, flat, or declining? (Use Google Trends as a proxy.)
- **Media coverage density:** How many publications covered this in the last 7/30/90 days?
- **Social conversation volume:** Is discussion accelerating on relevant platforms (LinkedIn, X/Twitter, industry forums)?
- **Competitive response rate:** How quickly are other thought leaders publishing on this?
- **Event triggers:** Is there a catalyst event (product launch, regulation, crisis) driving urgency?

### 2.4 Timing Decision Tree

Use this rapid-assessment flow for quick triage:

```
Is the topic trending RIGHT NOW (within this week)?
  YES -> Is the user's audience actively discussing it?
    YES -> Is there room for a differentiated take?
      YES -> GO (Priority: High, execute within 48 hours)
      NO  -> PASS (too saturated)
    NO  -> WAIT (monitor for audience pickup)
  NO -> Is it emerging with clear growth trajectory?
    YES -> PREPARE (draft now, publish when momentum builds)
    NO  -> ARCHIVE (revisit in 30 days or discard)
```

## 3. Opportunity Evaluation Criteria

This scoring matrix is adapted from the POEM Framework (Product Opportunity Evaluation Matrix), the ITONICS trend evaluation factors, and content scoring methodologies from the Content Marketing Institute.

### 3.1 The Opportunity Score Matrix

Evaluate every opportunity against these seven criteria. Rate each on a 1-5 scale.

| # | Criterion | What to Assess | Weight |
|---|-----------|---------------|--------|
| 1 | **Brand Alignment** | Does this fit the user's Brand DNA? Can the user speak on this authentically? Would the audience expect the user to have a perspective here? | x3 |
| 2 | **Audience Relevance** | Does the target audience care about this? Does it map to known personas? Will it drive engagement? | x3 |
| 3 | **Differentiation Potential** | Can the user offer a unique angle? Is there a gap in the current conversation that the user can fill? | x2 |
| 4 | **Timeliness** | Use the Timeliness Score from Section 2. Convert the 5-25 scale: 20-25=5, 14-19=4, 8-13=3, 5-7=1. | x2 |
| 5 | **Content Leverage** | Can this spawn multiple content pieces? Can it be repurposed across formats and platforms? | x1 |
| 6 | **Strategic Value** | Does this advance a quarterly goal or current campaign? Does it build toward a larger narrative? | x2 |
| 7 | **Execution Feasibility** | Can this be produced within the required timeline? Are the necessary resources and knowledge available? | x1 |

### 3.2 Scoring Calculation

```
Weighted Score = (Brand Alignment x 3) + (Audience Relevance x 3) +
                 (Differentiation x 2) + (Timeliness x 2) +
                 (Content Leverage x 1) + (Strategic Value x 2) +
                 (Execution Feasibility x 1)

Maximum possible score: 70
```

| Score Range | Recommendation |
|-------------|---------------|
| 56-70 | **Strong Go** — Prioritize immediately. This is a high-value opportunity. |
| 42-55 | **Go** — Solid opportunity. Schedule and execute within the timing window. |
| 28-41 | **Conditional** — Worth doing only if resources are available and timing is right. Consider a lighter-weight format. |
| 14-27 | **Pass** — Does not meet threshold. Archive for potential future relevance. |

### 3.3 Evaluation Principles

These principles guide how to apply the scoring matrix:

1. **Brand Alignment is non-negotiable.** If Brand Alignment scores 1 or 2, the opportunity is an automatic pass regardless of total score. Authenticity cannot be manufactured.

2. **Timeliness is a multiplier, not a standalone.** A timely topic with poor brand fit is worse than an evergreen topic with strong alignment. Timeliness amplifies good opportunities; it cannot redeem bad ones.

3. **Differentiation is the tiebreaker.** When two opportunities score similarly, choose the one where the user's unique perspective is strongest.

4. **Score honestly.** The matrix only works if ratings are realistic. A "3" is the baseline — it means adequate, not bad. Reserve "5" for genuinely exceptional fit.

5. **Reassess, don't anchor.** Scores change as conditions evolve. An opportunity that scored 30 last week might score 50 today if a triggering event occurs. Reassess active opportunities weekly.

### 3.4 Quick Evaluation Checklist

For rapid triage before running the full matrix, answer these five yes/no questions:

- [ ] Can the user speak on this authentically, without it feeling forced?
- [ ] Would the target audience care about the user's take on this?
- [ ] Is there something the user can say that isn't already being said?
- [ ] Is the timing window still open (Rising or early Peak phase)?
- [ ] Can this be executed within the available time and resources?

If fewer than 3 are "yes," skip the full evaluation — it is a pass.
If 3-4 are "yes," run the full matrix.
If all 5 are "yes," this is likely a strong opportunity — score it and act fast.
