## 1. Campaign Structure

### 1.1 The Campaign Architecture

A well-structured paid ad campaign has three layers:

```
CAMPAIGN (Budget + Objective)
  |
  +-- AD SET 1 (Audience + Placement + Schedule)
  |     +-- Ad Variation A
  |     +-- Ad Variation B
  |     +-- Ad Variation C
  |
  +-- AD SET 2 (Different Audience)
  |     +-- Ad Variation A
  |     +-- Ad Variation B
  |
  +-- AD SET 3 (Retargeting)
        +-- Ad Variation A
        +-- Ad Variation B
```

### 1.2 Campaign Objectives by Funnel Stage

| Funnel Stage | Objective | Platform Terminology | KPI |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Awareness** | Brand recognition, reach | Awareness, Reach, Video Views | CPM, Reach, Video View Rate |
| **Consideration** | Traffic, engagement | Traffic, Engagement, Video Views | CPC, CTR, Cost per Engagement |
| **Conversion** | Leads, sales | Conversions, Lead Gen, Catalog Sales | CPA, ROAS, Cost per Lead |
| **Retention** | Re-engagement | Retargeting, Custom Audiences | ROAS, LTV, Repeat Purchase Rate |

### 1.3 Campaign Types

| Type | Purpose | Budget % | Audience |
|------|---------|----------|----------|
| Prospecting | Reach new cold audiences | 40-60% | Lookalikes, interest-based, broad |
| Retargeting | Convert warm audiences | 20-30% | Website visitors, engagers, cart abandoners |
| Brand | Build awareness and recall | 10-20% | Broad, high-reach placements |
| Testing | Discover winning creative and audiences | 10-20% | Various test segments |

### 1.4 The 70/20/10 Creative Rule

- **70%** — Proven performers (scale what works)
- **20%** — Variations of winners (iterate on what works)
- **10%** — New concepts (test genuinely new ideas)

## 2. Targeting Methodology

### 2.1 Audience Targeting Layers

| Layer | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Demographic** | Age, gender, location, language | Broad reach campaigns |
| **Interest-based** | Hobbies, behaviors, affinities | Prospecting cold audiences |
| **Behavioral** | Purchase history, device usage, travel patterns | High-intent targeting |
| **Lookalike / Similar** | Audiences modeled on existing customers | Scaling winning audiences |
| **Custom / Retargeting** | Website visitors, email lists, engagers | Converting warm audiences |
| **Contextual** | Topic/keyword-based placement | Privacy-compliant prospecting |

### 2.2 Audience Segmentation Strategy

**Tier 1 — Hot audiences (highest priority):**
- Website visitors (last 7-30 days)
- Cart abandoners
- Email subscribers who clicked but did not convert
- Past customers (for upsell / retention)
- Video viewers who watched 75%+

**Tier 2 — Warm audiences:**
- Social media engagers (last 90 days)
- Email subscribers (opened but did not click)
- Website visitors (31-90 days)
- Video viewers who watched 25-75%

**Tier 3 — Cold audiences:**
- Lookalike audiences based on Tier 1
- Interest-based targeting
- Broad demographic targeting
- Competitor audience targeting (where available)

### 2.3 Privacy-First Targeting (2026)

With cookie deprecation and privacy regulations:
- **Zero-party data:** Data customers voluntarily share (quizzes, preference centers, surveys). Highest accuracy and trust.
- **First-party data:** Data from owned interactions (website visits, email engagement, CRM). Foundation of targeting.
- **Contextual targeting:** Place ads based on content topic, not user tracking. Privacy-compliant and growing.
- **AI-powered broad targeting:** Platforms like Meta's Advantage+ and Google's Performance Max use AI to find the best audiences with minimal manual targeting.

**Key insight:** Implement preference centers, interactive quizzes, and on-site experiences to capture explicit customer desires. Zero-party data provides personalization that respects privacy.

## 3. Budget Allocation

### 3.1 Budget Split by Campaign Type

| Campaign Type | % of Total Budget | Rationale |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Prospecting (cold) | 40-50% | Growth requires reaching new audiences |
| Retargeting (warm/hot) | 25-35% | Highest ROAS — convert known interest |
| Brand awareness | 10-15% | Long-term brand building |
| Testing | 10-20% | Discovery of new winning creative/audiences |

### 3.2 Budget Split by Platform

Allocate based on where your audience is, not where you are most comfortable:

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Audience concentration | Where does your ideal customer spend time? |
| Cost efficiency | What is the CPA/ROAS on each platform? |
| Content format fit | Does the platform support your strongest creative format? |
| Competition level | Higher competition = higher CPMs |
| Conversion path | How many steps from ad to conversion on this platform? |

**Starting allocation for most personal brands / solopreneurs:**
- Meta (Facebook + Instagram): 40-50%
- Google (Search + YouTube): 25-35%
- LinkedIn: 10-20% (if B2B focused)
- Emerging / testing: 5-10% (TikTok, CTV, etc.)

### 3.3 Testing Budget Rules

- Allocate 10-20% of total budget to experimentation
- Test one variable at a time (audience, creative, or placement — not all three)
- Run tests for minimum 3-5 days or until statistical significance
- Once a test produces a winner, allocate remaining budget to scale it
- Kill underperforming ads quickly — do not let them drain budget

### 3.4 Budget Scaling Strategy

**Vertical scaling:** Increase budget on winning campaigns gradually (20-30% increase every 3-5 days)
**Horizontal scaling:** Duplicate winning ad sets to new audience segments
**Rule:** Never more than double a budget overnight — it disrupts the algorithm's learning phase

## 4. Creative Best Practices

### 4.1 Creative Formats by Platform (2026)

| Platform | Top Format | Specs | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Meta (Feed) | Vertical video (9:16 or 4:5) | 1080x1350 or 1080x1920 | Raw, social-native outperforms polished |
| Meta (Reels) | Vertical video (9:16) | 1080x1920, <60s | Full-screen, fast-paced |
| Meta (Stories) | Vertical video/image | 1080x1920 | First 3s critical |
| Google Search | Text ads | Headlines: 30 char x3, Descriptions: 90 char x2 | Keyword relevance is king |
| YouTube | Horizontal video | 1920x1080, hook in 5s | Skippable ads need instant hooks |
| LinkedIn | Single image or document/carousel | 1200x627 or 1080x1080 | Professional, value-driven |
| TikTok | Vertical video (9:16) | 1080x1920, 15-60s | UGC-style, authentic |

### 4.2 The Creative Diversity Imperative (2026)

Meta's Andromeda algorithm is 100x faster at matching people to ads and handles 10,000x more ad variants in parallel. This means:
- **Feed the system variety, not volume** — Create many distinct creative variations
- **Creative diversity is a survival tactic** — Campaigns with 5+ distinct creative concepts outperform single-creative campaigns
- **Vertical, raw, social-native content** is the format Meta explicitly rewards
- **Static creative fatigue** is faster than ever — refresh creative every 2-4 weeks

### 4.3 Creative Testing Framework

**Level 1 — Concept testing:**
Test fundamentally different creative concepts (testimonial vs. demo vs. lifestyle vs. UGC)

**Level 2 — Element testing:**
Within winning concepts, test individual elements:
- Headlines / copy variations
- Visual style (photo vs. video vs. illustration)
- CTA placement and wording
- Color and design variations
- Hook / opening frame variations

**Level 3 — Iteration:**
Take the winning combination and create 5-10 variations with minor tweaks for freshness

### 4.4 Ad Copy Best Practices

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Headline | Benefit-first, specific, under 30 characters |
| Primary text | Hook in first line, problem-solution structure, social proof |
| CTA | Action-oriented, specific ("Get the Free Guide" not "Learn More") |
| Social proof | Numbers, testimonials, results — make claims credible |
| Urgency | Time limits, scarcity — only if genuine |
| Emoji | Sparingly on Meta/IG, never on LinkedIn or Google |

## 5. Platform Strategy

### 5.1 Platform Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Question to Ask |
|-----------|----------------|
| Audience presence | Is your ideal customer active here? |
| Ad format fit | Does the platform support your strongest content type? |
| Cost efficiency | What is the expected CPA or ROAS? |
| Competition | How crowded is your category on this platform? |
| Conversion path | How many steps from ad to conversion? |
| Data quality | What targeting and measurement capabilities exist? |
| Creative requirements | Can you produce the creative this platform demands? |

### 5.2 Platform Profiles (2026)

**Meta (Facebook + Instagram):**
- Largest ad platform for most personal brands
- AI-powered Advantage+ campaigns handle most targeting automatically
- Creative quality and diversity are the primary competitive advantage
- Best for: awareness, lead gen, e-commerce, community building
- Caveat: Requires high creative production velocity

**Google (Search + YouTube):**
- Captures high-intent audiences (people actively searching)
- Performance Max campaigns combine search, display, YouTube, and shopping
- Best for: direct response, high-intent lead capture, brand search defense
- Caveat: Competitive keywords can be expensive; requires ongoing keyword optimization

**LinkedIn:**
- Premium B2B audience with professional targeting (job title, company, industry)
- Higher CPMs but higher lead quality for B2B
- Best for: thought leadership, B2B lead gen, recruitment, professional services
- Caveat: Expensive — only viable if B2B revenue justifies the cost

**TikTok:**
- Younger demographic, high engagement, viral potential
- UGC-style creative outperforms polished ads
- Best for: brand awareness with Gen-Z/Millennial audiences, trend-riding
- Caveat: Conversion attribution is less mature than Meta/Google

**Connected TV (CTV) — Emerging in 2026:**
- Reaching cord-cutters on streaming platforms
- Video ads with programmatic targeting
- Growing rapidly — early movers get favorable rates
- Best for: broad awareness, premium brand positioning

### 5.3 Multi-Channel Strategy

- Map out a cross-platform customer journey
- Use awareness platforms (Meta, TikTok, CTV) at top of funnel
- Use intent platforms (Google Search) at bottom of funnel
- Retarget across platforms (someone who saw a YouTube ad, retargeted on Meta)
- Consistent messaging across channels, adapted to each platform's format

## 6. Campaign Optimization

### 6.1 Optimization Cadence

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Day 1-3 | Let ads run through learning phase — do not make changes |
| Day 3-5 | Review early metrics, kill obvious non-performers |
| Week 1 | Analyze results, pause underperformers, shift budget to winners |
| Week 2 | Introduce new creative variations for testing |
| Week 3-4 | Optimize audiences, bids, and placements based on data |
| Monthly | Full performance review, refresh creative, adjust strategy |

### 6.2 Key Optimization Levers

| Lever | When to Pull It |
|-------|----------------|
| Pause underperforming ads | CPA is 2x+ target after sufficient data |
| Increase budget on winners | ROAS consistently above target for 3+ days |
| Refresh creative | CTR dropping below benchmark or frequency above 3 |
| Narrow audience | CPM is high and conversion rate is low |
| Broaden audience | Campaign is limited by audience size and performing well |
| Adjust bidding | Consistently missing delivery or overspending |

## 7. Pre-Launch Checklist

Before launching any ad campaign, verify:

- [ ] Campaign objective aligns with business goal
- [ ] Target audience defined and segmented (hot, warm, cold)
- [ ] Budget allocated with clear spending limits per ad set
- [ ] Creative produced in correct specs for each platform
- [ ] 3+ creative variations per ad set for testing
- [ ] Landing page optimized and tested (load speed, mobile, CTA clarity)
- [ ] Tracking implemented (pixels, UTM parameters, conversion events)
- [ ] A/B test plan defined (what variable is being tested)
- [ ] Success KPIs defined (CPA target, ROAS target, etc.)
- [ ] Monitoring schedule set (who checks what, how often)
- [ ] Exit criteria defined (when to kill, when to scale)
