## 1. Content Analysis Dimensions

### 1.1 The Six Core Analysis Dimensions

Every content performance analysis should examine these six dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Measure | Key Questions |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Format** | Performance by content type | Which format (blog, social, thread, carousel, video, newsletter) gets the most engagement per piece? |
| **Topic** | Performance by theme/subject | Which themes consistently outperform? Which are underperforming? |
| **Tone** | Performance by voice/style | Does educational, personal, controversial, or motivational tone work best? |
| **Platform** | Performance by channel | Where does each format perform best? What is the cross-platform overlap? |
| **Timing** | Performance by when published | Day of week, time of day — when does engagement peak? |
| **Length** | Performance by content length | Short vs. long form — what is the engagement difference? |

### 1.2 Detailed Metric Categories

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Engagement rate: (total engagements / followers) x 100
- Time-on-page and scroll depth (50%+ scroll = genuine interest)
- Save rate and share rate (leading quality indicators — algorithmic amplifiers)
- Comment quality and sentiment (not just count)
- Click-through rate by platform and content type

**Reach and Distribution Metrics:**
- Organic reach vs. paid reach
- Impression decay rate: Speed of audience drop-off after 24 hours
- Audience overlap analysis: How many viewers are repeat from previous posts
- Viral coefficient: Shares per view/impression

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Content-attributed conversions (% of customers who encountered content before converting)
- Lead quality from content (MQLs generated per piece)
- Revenue attributed to content
- Email signups per content piece
- Click-to-conversion rate on CTAs

**SEO Metrics:**
- Organic traffic per piece
- Keyword rankings achieved
- Backlinks earned
- Time on page from organic search
- Bounce rate from organic search

### 1.3 Platform-Specific Metrics

| Platform | Primary Metrics | Secondary Metrics |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| YouTube | Watch time, average % viewed, session time | Thumbnail CTR, subscriber conversion, comments |
| TikTok | Completion rate, rewatch rate, share rate | Comments, follows from video, sound usage |
| Instagram | Save rate, share rate, profile visits | Reach, story exits, reel replays |
| LinkedIn | Engagement rate, comment quality, dwell time | Click-through to profile, connection requests |
| Twitter/X | Engagement rate, retweets, bookmark rate | Quote tweets, reply threads, follower gain |
| Blog | Time on page, scroll depth, organic traffic | Backlinks, returning visitors, pages per session |
| Newsletter | Open rate, click rate, reply rate | Forward rate, unsubscribe rate |

## 2. Pattern Identification

### 2.1 Pattern Discovery Framework

Systematically identify patterns by asking these questions across your data:

**Content Patterns:**
- Which 3 topics consistently appear in top 10% of performers?
- Which format + topic combinations outperform?
- Do longer or shorter posts perform better for specific topics?
- Which tone (educational / personal / controversial / motivational) correlates with highest engagement?

**Audience Patterns:**
- What content drives the most new followers vs. engaging existing ones?
- Which content attracts your ideal audience (not just any audience)?
- What content gets saved/bookmarked most (indicates reference value)?
- What content drives the most profile visits / website clicks?

**Timing Patterns:**
- Which day of week consistently outperforms?
- Is there a time-of-day pattern by platform?
- Are there seasonal patterns (monthly, quarterly)?
- How does engagement change during holidays or industry events?

**Distribution Patterns:**
- Which content type gets the most organic shares?
- What is the average lifespan of a post by format (viral spike vs. long tail)?
- How does cross-posting perform vs. platform-native content?

### 2.2 The "Content About X in Format Y on Platform Z" Template

The most actionable output of content performance analysis is completing this sentence:

> "Content about **[topic]** in **[format]** on **[platform]** at **[timing]** consistently **[outperforms/underperforms]** by **[X%]** compared to average."

**Examples:**
- "Content about personal failures in thread format on Twitter consistently outperforms by 3x compared to average engagement."
- "Educational carousel posts on LinkedIn on Tuesday mornings outperform all other content by 40%."
- "Long-form how-to blog posts about productivity drive 5x more organic traffic than opinion pieces."

### 2.3 Anomaly Detection

Flag for investigation:
- 30%+ engagement rate change vs. recent average
- Negative sentiment spike above 20% of mentions
- Unusual engagement increase (viral potential — amplify immediately)
- 50%+ traffic increase from content channels
- Significant conversion rate drops by content type

### 2.4 Headline and Hook Patterns

Track patterns in high-performing headlines:
- Specific numbers outperform vague language ("7 steps" > "several steps")
- Question formats outperform statements
- "How to" and "Why" formats consistently perform for educational content
- Personal/story-based hooks outperform generic openings
- Curiosity gaps drive higher CTR than direct benefit statements

**Source:** InfluenceFlow (2026); Outrank.so, "Content Performance Analysis: The Definitive Guide"; Content Managers, "How to Measure Content Performance in 2026"

## 3. Content Scoring Methodology

### 3.1 What Is Content Scoring

Content scoring assigns a numerical score to each piece of content based on its alignment with quality criteria and performance outcomes. It transforms the subjective idea of "quality" into an objective, measurable KPI.

### 3.2 Scoring Framework

**Pre-Publish Quality Score (0-100):**

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring Guide |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Topic relevance to audience | 20% | 0=off-topic, 50=somewhat relevant, 100=high-demand topic |
| Originality of insight | 15% | 0=rehashed, 50=some new perspective, 100=genuinely original |
| Brand voice alignment | 15% | 0=off-brand, 50=acceptable, 100=distinctly on-brand |
| Structural quality | 15% | 0=poorly structured, 50=adequate, 100=optimally structured |
| SEO optimization | 10% | 0=no optimization, 50=basic keywords, 100=fully optimized |
| Visual/formatting quality | 10% | 0=wall of text, 50=basic formatting, 100=excellent visual design |
| CTA clarity | 10% | 0=no CTA, 50=generic CTA, 100=specific, compelling CTA |
| Uniqueness factor | 5% | 0=anyone could write this, 50=some personality, 100=unmistakably "us" |

**Post-Publish Performance Score (0-100):**

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring Guide |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Engagement rate vs. average | 25% | 0=<25% of avg, 50=at avg, 100=>2x avg |
| Reach / impressions vs. average | 15% | 0=<25% of avg, 50=at avg, 100=>2x avg |
| Conversion / CTA clicks | 20% | 0=no conversions, 50=at avg, 100=>2x avg |
| Shares / saves | 15% | 0=none, 50=at avg, 100=>2x avg |
| Sentiment of responses | 10% | 0=negative, 50=neutral, 100=positive |
| Longevity (engagement over time) | 10% | 0=dead in 24h, 50=1 week, 100=evergreen |
| Revenue attribution | 5% | 0=none, 50=indirect, 100=direct revenue |

### 3.3 Composite Score

**Overall Content Score = (Pre-Publish Score x 0.3) + (Post-Publish Score x 0.7)**

| Score Range | Classification | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| 80-100 | Top Performer | Amplify, repurpose, create sequels, study patterns |
| 60-79 | Good | Optimize and learn — what could push it higher? |
| 40-59 | Average | Acceptable but not exceptional — identify weak dimensions |
| 20-39 | Underperforming | Analyze why — wrong topic, timing, format, or execution? |
| 0-19 | Poor | Investigate root cause — should this content type continue? |

### 3.4 Predictive Content Scoring (2026)

AI-powered predictive scoring can suggest: "Topic X with format Y, posted on day Z at time W will likely outperform alternatives by 30%."

Build your own predictive model by:
1. Scoring 50+ past content pieces retroactively
2. Identifying which pre-publish criteria best predict post-publish performance
3. Adjusting weights based on your data (not industry benchmarks)
4. Using the refined model to prioritize future content creation

**Source:** Contadu, "Content Scoring: How to Measure Quality Before You Publish"; TopicalMap.ai, "Content Scoring Tools Comparison 2026"; BruceClay, "How to Analyze Content Performance Metrics"

```

## Executive Summary
- Content output: [X] pieces across [Y] platforms
- Overall engagement trend: [Up/Down/Stable] vs. previous period
- Top performer: [Title] — [Key metric]
- Key insight: [One actionable finding]

## Top 5 Performers
| Rank | Content | Platform | Format | Key Metric | Score |
|------|---------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|

### Why They Worked

## Bottom 5 Performers
| Rank | Content | Platform | Format | Key Metric | Score |
|------|---------|----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| 1 | [Title] | [Platform] | [Format] | [Metric] | [Score] |
```

### 4.4 ROI Calculation

**Content ROI = (Revenue Attributed - Production Cost) / Production Cost x 100**

Example: $500 blog production cost generating $8,000 customer lifetime value = 1,400% ROI

Track production cost per piece (time + tools + promotion) and attribute revenue using multi-touch attribution models.
