## 1. Media Kit Structure

A professional media kit should contain seven core sections, kept to 1–3 scannable pages. Brands skim media kits — they do not read them. Every section must earn its space.

### 1.1 Bio & Positioning Statement

- 2–3 sentence maximum defining your niche and creative angle
- Professional headshot or brand photo
- Name, main social handle, and tagline
- Clear statement of content categories and brand-safety boundaries
- Avoid generic descriptors like "lifestyle creator" — be specific about who you serve

### 1.2 Audience Demographics

- Age, gender, geography, and interests breakdown
- Psychographic profile (values, pain points, aspirations)
- Platform-by-platform audience distribution
- Hyper-specific framing is more valuable: "92% women aged 28–35, interested in sustainable fashion" beats "mostly female, 25–40"
- A smaller, well-aligned audience is often more valuable than a large but unfocused one (CreatorsJet, 2026)

### 1.3 Performance Metrics

See Section 2 below for which metrics matter most. At minimum include:

- Follower counts across all active platforms with 6–12 month growth rates
- Average engagement rate per platform
- Monthly impressions and reach data
- Average views per video (for short-form creators, this matters more than followers)
- Growth trajectory shown visually if possible

### 1.4 Content Examples & Case Studies

- 3–5 top-performing content pieces with screenshots
- For each past brand collaboration, include:
  - Brand name and deliverable description
  - Reach / impressions generated
  - Engagement rate on the sponsored content
  - Click-throughs, promo code redemptions, or sales data if available
- Show consistent performance, not just viral outliers

### 1.5 Sponsorship Packages / Rate Card

See Section 3 below for tiered package design. Include:

- Per-deliverable rates (single post, Reel, TikTok, YouTube integration, Story set)
- Bundled packages at 2–3 tiers
- Add-on pricing (usage rights, exclusivity, whitelisting, affiliate commission)
- "Starting at" framing to anchor without boxing yourself in

### 1.6 Testimonials & Social Proof

- Direct quotes from past brand partners
- Logos of notable collaborators (if permitted)
- Screenshots of positive brand feedback
- Any press mentions, awards, or speaking engagements

### 1.7 Contact Information

- Professional business email (not personal)
- Management contact if applicable
- Response time expectation (e.g. "Replies within 48 hours")
- Link to live media kit or portfolio site

## 2. Metrics That Matter to Sponsors

### 2.1 The Engagement Rate Shift

In 2026, 73% of brands prioritize authentic audience data over follower counts when evaluating creator partnerships (Influencer Marketing Hub, 2026 State of Influencer Marketing). The question has shifted from "who has the biggest audience" to "whose audience actually cares."

**Core formula:**
```
Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Followers × 100
```

**Platform benchmarks (2026):**

| Platform | Micro (10K–100K) | Mid (100K–1M) | Macro (1M+) |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|-------------|
| Instagram | 2–4% | 1–2% | 0.5–1% |
| TikTok | 3–6% | 2–4% | 1–3% |
| LinkedIn | 2–4% (personal) | 1–2% | 0.5–1.5% |
| X/Twitter | 1–3% (niche) | 0.5–2% | — |

Source: InfluenceFlow 2026 Engagement Rate Metrics & Benchmarking Guide

### 2.2 Quality Signals Over Vanity Metrics

Not all engagement is equal. Brands weight interactions in this priority order:

1. **Shares** — highest signal; a single share can be worth 20 likes. Users only share content they believe is valuable enough for their own network.
2. **Saves** — saved content is 40% more likely to drive conversions than liked content (Later, 2026).
3. **Comments** — weighted 3x more than likes in Instagram's algorithm. Comment quality and sentiment matter more than count.
4. **Click-throughs** — direct action metric; most important when the goal is traffic or conversions.
5. **Likes** — lowest-weight signal, but still baseline proof of reach.

### 2.3 Audience Quality Metrics

Sponsors increasingly evaluate:

- **Audience authenticity** — Are engagers real profiles in the target demographic?
- **Audience alignment** — Do engagers match the brand's customer profile?
- **Sentiment analysis** — Ratio of positive to negative interactions
- **Audience lifetime value (ALV)** — Estimated total revenue from one follower over time. High ALV audiences (premium, B2B, high-income) justify higher partnership rates.

### 2.4 Conversion Data to Include

When available, these metrics dramatically strengthen a media kit:

- Promo code redemption rates
- Affiliate link click-through and conversion rates
- Swipe-up / link-in-bio click rates
- Email list signup rates from sponsored content
- Direct sales attribution data

Key benchmark: A 50,000-follower account with 8% engagement often outperforms a 500,000-follower account with 1% engagement in actual brand value (InfluenceFlow, 2026).

### 2.5 Platform-Specific Metric Priorities

**Short-form (TikTok / Instagram Reels):**
- Average views per video over follower count
- Viewer-to-follower ratio (top creators: 50K followers generating 300K+ views)
- Completion rate

**Long-form (YouTube / Podcasts):**
- Watch time and audience retention (70%+ is excellent)
- Average downloads per episode (podcasts)
- Monthly AdSense data if monetized

**Newsletter / Email:**
- Open rates (40%+ is strong)
- Click-through rates
- Subscriber growth rate

**LinkedIn / B2B:**
- Post engagement and article reach
- Speaking engagements and publications
- Network quality over size

## 3. Tiered Package Design

### 3.1 Why Tiers Work

Tiered packages give brands entry points at different budget levels. They also anchor pricing — the middle tier typically converts best when positioned between a modest starter and a premium option (the "decoy effect").

For creators, 2–3 tiers is the sweet spot. More than that creates decision fatigue and dilutes perceived value.

### 3.2 Recommended Tier Structure

**Starter / Bronze — Entry-Level Access**
- Single deliverable (one post, one Reel, one Story set)
- Standard usage rights (creator's channels only, 30 days)
- Basic performance report post-campaign
- Price at 15–25% of your top tier

**Pro / Silver — Most Popular (flag this as "recommended")**
- Multi-deliverable bundle (e.g., 1 Reel + 2 Stories + 1 static post)
- Cross-platform distribution
- Extended usage rights (90 days)
- Detailed performance report with engagement breakdown
- Price at 40–60% of your top tier

**Premium / Gold — Full Partnership**
- Campaign-level collaboration (3–6 deliverables over 1–3 months)
- Creative strategy input and content planning
- Full usage rights (12 months, paid media whitelisting)
- Exclusivity within category
- Comprehensive ROI report with conversion data
- This is your anchor price — set it at market rate for your tier

### 3.3 Pricing Guidelines

**Baseline models (industry standards):**
- Traditional follower-based: ~$500 per 10K followers per post
- Engagement-based: Engagement rate x followers x platform multiplier

**Average rates by creator size (2026):**

| Creator Tier | Per-Post Range |
|-------------|----------------|
| Nano (1K–10K) | $100–$500 |
| Micro (10K–100K) | $500–$5,000 |
| Mid-tier (100K–500K) | $5,000–$20,000 |
| Macro (500K–1M) | $20,000–$50,000 |
| Mega (1M+) | $50,000+ |

Source: InfluenceFlow 2026 Creator Media Kits Guide

**Content-type rate adjustments:**
- Instagram static post: base rate
- Instagram Reel (with production): 1.5–2x base
- TikTok video: 0.6–0.7x Instagram rate
- YouTube integration: 2–3x base
- Story set (3–5 frames): 0.5x base

**Bundle discount structure:**
- 3-deliverable package: 10% discount
- 6-deliverable package: 15% discount
- Ongoing retainer (monthly): 20–25% discount

### 3.4 Premium Add-On Pricing

Present these as optional line items alongside your tiers:

| Add-On | Typical Premium |
|--------|----------------|
| Usage rights extension (beyond 30 days) | +50–100% of base rate |
| Exclusivity clause (category lock-out) | +30–50% |
| Whitelisting / paid media rights | +25–50% |
| Affiliate commission | 5–10% of sales |
| Rush delivery (under 48 hours) | +25–50% |
| Revision rounds (beyond 1) | +10–15% per round |

### 3.5 Pricing Presentation Tips

- Use "starting at" language to anchor without locking in
- Lead with value delivered, not cost charged
- Present the mid-tier as "Most Popular" to guide selection
- Include a "Custom" option for enterprise / long-term partnerships
- Never apologize for pricing — confidence signals professionalism

## 4. Design & Format Best Practices

### 4.1 Format Options

| Format | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| PDF (under 3MB) | Cold email outreach, quick sharing |
| Interactive web page | Scheduled meetings, always-up-to-date |
| Video intro (30–60 sec) | Maximum impact, personal touch |
| Loom walkthrough | Custom outreach to high-value brands |
| Live auto-generated kit | Automated metric updates, highest conversion (4x vs static PDF — CreatorsJet, 2026) |

### 4.2 Design Principles

- **F-pattern layout**: Name / photo / top metric at the top — that is where eyes land first
- **Mobile-first**: 60%+ of media kits are viewed on phones
- 300 DPI minimum for images
- Consistent typography and color palette aligned with personal brand
- Generous whitespace — cramped kits feel amateur
- 1–3 pages maximum; longer is not better

### 4.3 Update Cadence

- **Minimum**: Quarterly
- **Recommended**: Monthly
- **Trigger immediate update when**: Hitting a follower milestone, engagement rate changes significantly, completing a notable brand partnership, launching on a new platform

## 5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Stale metrics** — Outdated numbers destroy credibility instantly
2. **Generic audience descriptions** — "Lifestyle content" tells brands nothing
3. **Pricing ambiguity** — Unclear what is included in each deliverable
4. **Missing usage rights terms** — Brands need to know what they can do with the content
5. **Only vanity metrics** — Follower count without engagement context is a red flag
6. **No case studies** — Claims without proof are ignored
7. **Mobile-unfriendly PDFs** — Over half of brand contacts will open on mobile
8. **Trying to appeal to everyone** — Specificity wins deals; generality loses them
