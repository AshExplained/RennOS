## 1. Escalation Decision Tree

Use this framework to triage every inbound social signal before crafting a response. The goal is to route each event to the correct response level — fast.

### 1.1 Traffic Light Classification

| Level | Color | Description | Who Handles | Response Time |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | GREEN | Routine mentions, positive comments, simple questions | Social media manager | Within 4 hours |
| 2 | YELLOW | Negative feedback, minor complaints, emerging patterns | Social + department lead review | Within 2 hours |
| 3 | ORANGE | Widespread service issues, trending negative hashtag, influencer complaint | Cross-functional team (social, PR, product) | Within 1 hour |
| 4 | RED | Viral boycott, legal exposure, executive misconduct, safety issue | Executive + legal approval required | Within 30 minutes |

Source: Sprinklr's three-tier system adapted to four levels for granularity.

### 1.2 Decision Tree — When to Engage, Escalate, or Ignore

```
INCOMING SIGNAL
│
├─ Is it spam, bot activity, or obvious trolling?
│   ├─ YES → IGNORE. Block if harassing. Do not engage.
│   └─ NO ↓
│
├─ Is it a genuine question or positive mention?
│   ├─ YES → ENGAGE directly. Thank, answer, amplify.
│   └─ NO ↓
│
├─ Is it negative feedback or a complaint?
│   ├─ YES ↓
│   │   ├─ Is it about a real product/service issue?
│   │   │   ├─ YES ↓
│   │   │   │   ├─ Is it gaining traction (shares, replies, media pickup)?
│   │   │   │   │   ├─ YES → ESCALATE to ORANGE/RED. Notify cross-functional team.
│   │   │   │   │   └─ NO → ENGAGE with empathy. Acknowledge, redirect to DM/support.
│   │   │   └─ NO (misunderstanding or misinformation) ↓
│   │   │       └─ ENGAGE with correction. Polite, factual, non-defensive.
│   │   │
│   │   └─ Is the person a known troll or bad-faith actor?
│   │       ├─ YES → IGNORE. Do not feed the fire.
│   │       └─ NO → ENGAGE. Empathy first, solution second.
│   └─ NO ↓
│
├─ Is it a viral moment (positive or ambiguous)?
│   ├─ YES → EVALUATE opportunity. See Viral Moment playbook (§2.1).
│   └─ NO ↓
│
├─ Is it a crisis (safety, legal, reputational)?
│   ├─ YES → ESCALATE immediately to RED. See Crisis playbook (§2.2 / §2.5).
│   └─ NO ↓
│
└─ Is it a collaboration or partnership signal?
    ├─ YES → EVALUATE. See Collaboration playbook (§2.3).
    └─ NO → LOG and monitor. No action needed now.
```

### 1.3 Escalation Response Protocol

Once an event is classified ORANGE or RED:

1. **Pause** all scheduled content on affected platforms
2. **Notify** the crisis communications lead within 15 minutes
3. **Gather facts** — what happened, when, who is affected, which platforms
4. **Draft** a holding statement (acknowledge issue, express empathy, promise update)
5. **Approve** via the designated approval chain (never publish without sign-off)
6. **Publish** the initial response on the primary platform where the issue lives
7. **Monitor** sentiment, reply volume, and media pickup in real-time
8. **Update** stakeholders every 30-60 minutes until resolved

### 1.4 Three Response Strategies for Negative Interactions

| Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Engage with Empathy** | Genuine complaint, real frustration | "I understand how frustrating this must be. Let me look into this personally." |
| **Acknowledge & Redirect** | Angry customer, complex issue | "We're sorry about your experience. Please DM us so we can resolve this directly." |
| **Ignore & Block** | Trolls, harassment, bad-faith actors | Do not respond. Block if behavior violates community guidelines. |

Source: B Squared Media escalation scripts framework.

## 2. Scenario Playbooks

### 2.1 Viral Moment

**Definition:** Your content, brand, or a mention of you goes unexpectedly viral — positive, neutral, or ambiguous.

#### Phase 1: Assess (First 30 minutes)
- Identify the source and nature of the virality
- Determine sentiment: positive, neutral, or mixed
- Estimate trajectory: growing, peaking, or declining
- Check: Does this align with brand values? Is there risk?

#### Phase 2: Decide (30-60 minutes)
- **Ride it** (positive/neutral, brand-aligned): Move to Phase 3
- **Redirect it** (ambiguous, could go either way): Prepare a statement that steers the narrative
- **Distance from it** (misaligned or risky): Do not engage publicly; prepare a statement only if directly asked

#### Phase 3: Capitalize (1-4 hours)
- **Content response:** Create a response post that is authentic, timely, and adds value — not just "thanks for the love"
- **Engagement blitz:** Reply to top comments, quote-retweet with personality, engage creators who amplified it
- **Cross-platform amplification:** Repurpose the moment for other platforms (e.g., viral tweet → Instagram Story → LinkedIn reflection)
- **Community involvement:** Invite your audience into the moment — polls, reactions, UGC prompts

#### Phase 4: Sustain (1-7 days)
- **Follow-up content:** Create 2-3 pieces that build on the momentum (behind-the-scenes, "how it happened," lessons learned)
- **Relationship building:** DM and connect with key people who amplified the moment
- **Document:** Log what worked, what the trigger was, and what you would replicate

#### Timing Rules
- Peak engagement window: First 2-4 hours on X/Twitter, first 6-12 hours on Instagram/TikTok, first 24 hours on LinkedIn
- Responding after the moment dies is worse than not responding at all
- Authenticity beats speed — never post something forced just to ride a wave

#### Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Respond with genuine personality | Force a brand plug into the moment |
| Engage with people who amplified you | Ignore the people who made it happen |
| Create follow-up content while attention is high | Let the moment pass without capturing it |
| Credit original creators if relevant | Claim credit for organic community moments |
| Monitor for sentiment shift | Assume positive virality can't turn negative |

### 2.2 Controversy / Crisis

**Definition:** Your brand is facing public criticism, backlash, or a PR incident that threatens reputation.

#### Severity Assessment Matrix

| Signal | Low (GREEN) | Medium (YELLOW/ORANGE) | High (RED) |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|------------|
| Volume of mentions | < 50 in 1 hour | 50-500 in 1 hour | 500+ in 1 hour |
| Sentiment | Mixed | Predominantly negative | Overwhelmingly negative |
| Media involvement | None | Industry blogs/niche media | Mainstream media pickup |
| Influencer amplification | None | Minor influencers | Major influencers/public figures |
| Legal/safety risk | None | Potential | Active |

#### Response Framework: ALER (Acknowledge, Listen, Explain, Resolve)

**Step 1 — Acknowledge (within 1 hour)**
- Post a brief statement: "We're aware of [issue] and are looking into it"
- Do NOT go silent — silence creates space for speculation
- Do NOT delete the original post/comment unless it violates safety guidelines
- Pause all scheduled content

**Step 2 — Listen (ongoing)**
- Monitor all platforms for volume, sentiment, and key narratives
- Identify the core complaint — what are people actually upset about?
- Track which voices are amplifying the issue
- Document everything for post-crisis review

**Step 3 — Explain (within 4-24 hours, depending on severity)**
- Share context without being defensive
- Take responsibility where warranted — partial accountability is worse than full
- Be specific about what happened and why
- Use the right platform: LinkedIn for professional/detailed statements, X for real-time updates, Instagram/Facebook for humanizing responses

**Step 4 — Resolve (24-72 hours)**
- Announce concrete corrective actions
- Provide a timeline for changes
- Follow up publicly on commitments made
- Thank community members who provided constructive feedback

#### Recovery Metrics
- Track sentiment return to baseline (target: within 2 weeks)
- Monitor follower count changes
- Measure engagement rate recovery
- Document lessons learned and update crisis playbook

#### Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Respond quickly with empathy | Go silent or hope it blows over |
| Take genuine responsibility | Blame customers or external factors |
| Communicate on the platform where the crisis lives | Force people to a different channel for your statement |
| Be transparent about corrective actions | Make vague promises without specifics |
| Follow up on commitments publicly | Delete negative comments (unless safety violations) |
| Conduct a post-mortem | Move on without documenting lessons |

### 2.3 Collaboration Opportunity

**Definition:** An inbound or outbound opportunity to partner with another creator, brand, or organization for mutual benefit.

#### Evaluation Framework (VFAR)

| Criteria | Question | Score 1-5 |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **V**alue Alignment | Does their brand align with our values and positioning? | |
| **F**it | Does their audience overlap with or complement ours? | |
| **A**uthenticity | Would this partnership feel natural, not forced? | |
| **R**each | Does this meaningfully expand our reach or credibility? | |

**Threshold:** Total score of 14+ = proceed. 10-13 = consider with conditions. Below 10 = decline gracefully.

#### Inbound Collaboration Protocol

1. **Acknowledge** within 24 hours (even if just to say you're reviewing)
2. **Research** their brand, audience, recent content, and any controversies
3. **Score** using VFAR framework
4. **Propose** terms: scope, deliverables, timeline, promotion plan, attribution
5. **Draft** collaboration content for approval before publishing
6. **Execute** with cross-promotion on both sides
7. **Measure** results: reach, engagement, follower growth, sentiment

#### Outbound Collaboration Protocol

1. **Identify** targets through social listening, industry mapping, audience overlap analysis
2. **Warm up** by engaging with their content authentically for 2-4 weeks before pitching
3. **Pitch** with value-first framing: what you bring to them, not what you want from them
4. **Customize** the pitch to reference specific content of theirs you admire
5. **Propose** a low-commitment first collaboration (guest post, joint live, content swap)
6. **Follow up** once after 5-7 days if no response; then stop

#### Collaboration Formats by Platform

| Platform | Best Formats |
|----------|-------------|
| LinkedIn | Co-authored articles, joint LinkedIn Live, mutual endorsements |
| Instagram | Collab posts (dual-credited), joint Reels, Story takeovers |
| X/Twitter | Twitter Spaces co-hosting, thread collaborations, mutual amplification |
| TikTok | Duets, joint challenges, creator series |
| YouTube | Guest appearances, cross-channel features, collaborative series |
| Podcast | Guest spots, crossover episodes, panel discussions |

#### Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Research thoroughly before engaging | Accept every collaboration offer |
| Lead with value to the partner | Make it only about what you get |
| Set clear expectations on deliverables | Leave terms ambiguous |
| Cross-promote authentically | Phone in your promotion of the partner |
| Measure and share results with the partner | Forget to follow up post-collaboration |

### 2.4 Product Launch

**Definition:** Coordinated social media campaign to announce, build anticipation for, and drive engagement around a new product, feature, or offering.

#### Launch Timeline Framework

**Phase 1: Seed (4-6 weeks before launch)**
- Drop subtle hints in existing content — behind-the-scenes, "something's coming"
- Engage your most active community members privately (beta testers, superfans)
- Create a content bank: 15-20 pieces ready to deploy across phases
- Brief any collaboration partners on timing and messaging

**Phase 2: Tease (2-4 weeks before launch)**
- Countdown content with escalating reveals
- Platform-specific teaser formats:
  - Instagram: Stories countdown sticker, Reels with blurred reveals
  - X/Twitter: Cryptic tweets, polls about the product category
  - LinkedIn: Thought leadership on the problem your product solves
  - TikTok: "Day in the life" building/creating the product
- Encourage speculation — engagement thrives on curiosity

**Phase 3: Launch (Day 0)**
- **Primary platform post** with full announcement (optimized for that platform's format)
- **Cross-platform cascade** within 2 hours — adapt the message, don't copy-paste
- **Engagement blitz**: Reply to every comment in the first 4 hours
- **Influencer/partner amplification**: Coordinated posts from collaborators
- **Community activation**: UGC prompt, challenge, or contest tied to the launch
- **Live component**: Consider a launch-day Live session (LinkedIn Live, Instagram Live, X Spaces)

**Phase 4: Sustain (1-4 weeks after launch)**
- Share customer/user reactions and testimonials
- Create "how to" and "tips" content around the product
- Address common questions publicly (builds trust and SEO)
- Highlight unexpected use cases from the community
- Run a post-launch AMA or Q&A

#### Launch Day Engagement Cadence

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T-1 hour | Final teaser: "1 hour..." |
| T-0 | Main announcement post (primary platform) |
| T+15 min | Cross-post to secondary platforms |
| T+30 min | Begin responding to all comments |
| T+1 hour | Share to Stories/ephemeral content |
| T+2 hours | Partner/influencer posts go live |
| T+4 hours | First engagement summary post ("The response has been...") |
| T+24 hours | Behind-the-scenes / making-of content |
| T+48 hours | First user testimonial/reaction roundup |

#### Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Build anticipation with a multi-phase approach | Drop a product announcement cold |
| Engage every commenter on launch day | Post and walk away |
| Adapt content per platform | Copy-paste the same announcement everywhere |
| Have a content bank ready for 2+ weeks post-launch | Exhaust all your content on day one |
| Celebrate your community's response publicly | Make it only about the product, not the people |

### 2.5 Community Crisis

**Definition:** An event within your community (toxic behavior, misinformation spreading among members, community fracture, loss of a community member, external attack on community members) that requires careful handling.

#### Community Crisis Types

| Type | Example | Severity |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Toxic behavior** | Harassment between members, bullying | ORANGE |
| **Misinformation** | False claims spreading within community spaces | YELLOW |
| **Community fracture** | Disagreement splitting the community into factions | ORANGE |
| **External attack** | Coordinated trolling or brigading from outside | RED |
| **Sensitive event** | Loss of a community member, tragedy affecting members | RED |
| **Trust violation** | Leaked private information, broken promises | RED |

#### Response Framework: SHIELD

**S — Secure the space**
- Remove harmful content immediately (harassment, doxxing, threats)
- Temporarily restrict posting if the situation is escalating
- Document everything before taking action

**H — Hear the community**
- Listen before speaking — understand all perspectives
- Reach out privately to key community members for their read on the situation
- Do not dismiss concerns, even if you disagree

**I — Inform transparently**
- Share what you know and what you're doing about it
- Be honest about what you don't know yet
- Set expectations for next update

**E — Enforce consistently**
- Apply community guidelines equally, regardless of member status
- Explain the reasoning behind moderation decisions
- Document enforcement actions

**L — Learn and adapt**
- Conduct post-crisis community check-in
- Update community guidelines if gaps are identified
- Share what changed as a result of the crisis

**D — Document**
- Record the timeline, decisions made, outcomes
- Update the crisis playbook with new patterns
- Note what worked and what didn't for next time

#### Community-Specific Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately to RED if any of these are present:
- Physical safety threats to any community member
- Doxxing or sharing of private information
- Coordinated harassment campaigns
- Legal liability (defamation, IP theft, discrimination)
- Media involvement or potential media involvement
- Mental health crisis signals from a community member

#### Do's and Don'ts
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Prioritize member safety above all else | Prioritize engagement metrics over wellbeing |
| Communicate proactively and transparently | Let rumors fill the information vacuum |
| Enforce rules consistently | Play favorites with high-profile members |
| Create space for community healing | Rush "back to normal" before the community is ready |
| Update guidelines based on lessons learned | Treat the crisis as a one-off without systemic changes |

## 3. Engagement Best Practices by Scenario Type

### 3.1 Universal Principles (All Scenarios)

1. **Speed matters but accuracy matters more.** First response should come fast; full response should come right.
2. **Empathy is not optional.** Every interaction involves a human. Lead with understanding.
3. **Brand voice stays consistent** even in crisis. You can be serious without being corporate, empathetic without being sycophantic.
4. **Document everything.** Every scenario is a case study for the next one.
5. **Algorithms reward engagement quality.** In 2026, meaningful conversations outperform vanity metrics across all platforms.
6. **The 80/20 rule applies.** Spend 80% of social time engaging with others, 20% posting your own content.
7. **Community is a moat.** Strong communities act as buffers during crises — loyal members defend the brand without prompting.

### 3.2 Response Time Benchmarks

| Scenario | Target First Response | Target Full Response |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Positive mention / question | 4 hours | Same day |
| Negative feedback | 2 hours | 4-8 hours |
| Active controversy | 30 minutes | 1-4 hours |
| Safety / legal crisis | 15 minutes | 1 hour |
| Viral moment (positive) | 30 minutes | 2 hours |
| Collaboration inbound | 24 hours | 48-72 hours |
| Product launch comments | Immediate (during launch window) | 1 hour |

### 3.3 Platform-Specific Engagement Rules

**LinkedIn**
- Professional, thoughtful tone
- Long-form responses are valued — don't be afraid to write substantive replies
- Best for: thought leadership, detailed statements, professional collaborations
- Engagement peak: Tuesday-Thursday, business hours

**X / Twitter**
- Concise, personality-forward
- Speed is paramount — the platform moves fast
- Best for: real-time updates, quick engagement, trending conversations
- Engagement peak: Consistent throughout weekdays

**Instagram**
- Visual-first, authentic, warm tone
- Stories for ephemeral/time-sensitive content, Reels for reach
- Best for: behind-the-scenes, humanizing the brand, community building
- Engagement peak: Evenings and weekends

**TikTok**
- Casual, trend-aware, creator-style voice
- Video replies to comments perform exceptionally well
- Best for: reaching new audiences, trend-jacking, authenticity plays
- Engagement peak: Evenings, especially Thursday-Sunday

**Facebook**
- Community-oriented, inclusive tone
- Groups are more powerful than Pages for engagement
- Best for: community building, long-form discussion, local audiences
- Engagement peak: Midday and early evening

### 3.4 Content Structure for Engagement

Every engagement-focused post should follow the **Hook-Value-CTA** framework:

1. **Hook** — Stop the scroll with a bold claim, surprising statistic, or relatable pain point
2. **Value** — Deliver on the hook's promise with actionable insight
3. **CTA** — Tell the reader exactly what to do next (comment, share, save, click)

### 3.6 Scenario Quick-Reference Decision Matrix

| Scenario | First Action | Key Risk | Success Metric |
|----------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| Viral moment | Assess sentiment and brand alignment | Positive virality turning negative | Sustained engagement lift for 7+ days |
| Controversy | Acknowledge publicly within 1 hour | Silence interpreted as guilt | Sentiment return to baseline within 2 weeks |
| Collaboration | Research partner thoroughly | Brand misalignment discovered post-launch | Measurable audience growth from partner's base |
| Product launch | Execute teaser sequence per timeline | Launch falling flat due to no buildup | Day-1 engagement rate 2x above baseline |
| Community crisis | Secure member safety immediately | Losing trust of core community members | Community retention rate above 90% |
