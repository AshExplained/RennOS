# Social Media Platform Audit — Best Practices & Benchmarks

> Reference document for the platform-audit skill. Updated March 2026.
> Read this before conducting any platform audit to ground your analysis in current methodology and data.

---

## Part 1: Platform Audit Dimensions

A rigorous audit evaluates six core dimensions. Score each 1-10 with specific evidence.

### 1. Profile Optimization

Assess the completeness and quality of the profile itself.

| Element | What to check |
|---------|---------------|
| **Username/handle** | Memorable, consistent across platforms, matches brand name |
| **Display name** | Clear, includes relevant keywords where appropriate |
| **Bio/About** | Concise value proposition, target audience clear, includes CTA |
| **Profile image** | High-res, recognizable at small sizes, consistent across platforms |
| **Banner/cover** | On-brand, current (not outdated promos), correct dimensions |
| **Links** | Working links to website, landing page, or link-in-bio tool |
| **Contact info** | Accessible where the platform allows it |
| **Verification** | Verified badge where available and relevant |
| **SEO/discoverability** | Keywords in bio, alt text usage, hashtag strategy in profile |
| **Pinned content** | Best-performing or most strategic content pinned/featured |

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: Every element optimized, consistent with brand DNA, discoverable
- 7-8: Most elements present, minor gaps (e.g., outdated banner, missing CTA)
- 5-6: Functional but unoptimized — missing keywords, weak bio, inconsistent imagery
- 3-4: Significant gaps — broken links, no CTA, brand mismatch
- 1-2: Profile is incomplete or abandoned

### 2. Content Performance

Analyze the last 30-90 days of content output.

**Quantitative metrics:**
- Total posts in audit period
- Average reach/impressions per post
- Top-performing posts (by engagement, reach, saves, shares)
- Worst-performing posts (identify patterns)
- Content format breakdown (text, image, video, carousel, stories, etc.)
- Posting frequency and cadence consistency

**Qualitative assessment:**
- Voice and tone alignment with Brand DNA
- Visual consistency (colors, fonts, imagery style)
- Content pillar coverage — are all brand themes represented?
- Hook quality — do first lines/frames capture attention?
- CTA effectiveness — do posts drive desired actions?
- Storytelling quality — do posts create connection, not just inform?

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: Consistent output, strong performance trends, clear content strategy visible
- 7-8: Regular posting, some high performers, but inconsistent quality or gaps in pillars
- 5-6: Sporadic posting, mediocre engagement, unclear strategy
- 3-4: Infrequent, off-brand, or low-quality content
- 1-2: Essentially inactive or content is damaging to brand

### 3. Engagement Quality

Go beyond vanity metrics. Measure the depth and quality of interactions.

**Metrics to evaluate:**
- Engagement rate (calculate per platform formula — see benchmarks below)
- Comment quality — are people having real conversations or just dropping emojis?
- Share/repost ratio — indicator of content value beyond the existing audience
- Save ratio — indicator of reference-worthy content (Instagram, TikTok)
- Reply rate from the account — is the brand actively engaging back?
- DM volume and quality (if accessible)
- Sentiment analysis — positive, neutral, negative comment ratio

**Engagement depth tiers:**
1. **Passive** — Likes, views (low effort from audience)
2. **Active** — Comments, shares, saves (moderate effort)
3. **Invested** — DMs, UGC, advocacy, repeat engagement (high effort)

Healthy accounts show a mix across all three tiers, with growing ratios in tiers 2 and 3.

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: High engagement rate vs. benchmarks, quality conversations, active community
- 7-8: Above-average engagement, some quality interactions, room to deepen
- 5-6: Average engagement, mostly passive (likes only), limited conversation
- 3-4: Below-benchmark engagement, spam comments, or echo chamber
- 1-2: Near-zero engagement or predominantly negative sentiment

### 4. Audience Alignment

Determine whether the followers match the target audience defined in brand strategy.

**Check against audience personas:**
- Demographics — age, location, language, gender (where available)
- Psychographics — interests, values, professional roles
- Follower authenticity — bot/fake follower ratio (tools: SparkToro, HypeAuditor)
- Follower-to-following ratio — does the account attract or chase?
- Follower growth source — organic vs. paid vs. giveaway-driven

**Key questions:**
- Would these followers buy/hire/engage with the brand's offerings?
- Are the most engaged followers in the target persona?
- Is growth attracting more of the right people or diluting the audience?

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: Audience closely matches target personas, high authenticity, organic growth
- 7-8: Mostly aligned, some off-target segments, authentic
- 5-6: Partially aligned, significant non-target followers, unclear growth quality
- 3-4: Misaligned audience, high bot ratio, or growth from non-strategic sources
- 1-2: Audience bears no resemblance to target market

### 5. Competitor Comparison

Benchmark against 3-5 direct competitors or aspirational accounts in the same niche.

**Compare across:**
- Follower count and growth rate
- Engagement rate (normalized by follower count)
- Content frequency and format mix
- Content themes and positioning
- Audience overlap (tools: SparkToro, Social Blade)
- Platform-specific features adoption (e.g., Reels, Threads, LinkedIn newsletters)

**Identify:**
- Content gaps — what competitors cover that the audited account does not
- Format gaps — what content types competitors use successfully
- Engagement gaps — where competitors outperform and why
- Differentiation opportunities — what the audited account does that no competitor does

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: Outperforming competitors on key metrics, clear differentiation
- 7-8: Competitive, on par with peers, some areas of advantage
- 5-6: Middle of the pack, no clear differentiation
- 3-4: Underperforming most competitors on most metrics
- 1-2: Significantly behind all competitors

### 6. Growth Trajectory

Assess the direction and velocity of account growth over time.

**Metrics:**
- Follower growth rate (monthly, quarterly, YoY)
- Engagement rate trend (improving, flat, declining)
- Reach/impression trend
- Content performance trend — are newer posts outperforming older ones?
- Virality — any breakout content? Frequency of above-average performers?

**Growth health indicators:**
- Steady organic growth > spike-and-drop patterns
- Engagement rate growing alongside follower count (not inversely)
- Diversified growth sources (search, explore, shares, not just one channel)

**Scoring guide:**
- 9-10: Strong upward trajectory across all growth metrics, compounding momentum
- 7-8: Positive growth trend, some plateaus but overall healthy
- 5-6: Flat — no decline but no meaningful growth
- 3-4: Declining metrics, losing followers or engagement
- 1-2: Account is shrinking or stagnant with no recovery signals

## Part 2: Engagement Benchmarks by Platform (2025-2026 Data)

Use these benchmarks to contextualize the audited account's performance. Always compare against the relevant platform, follower tier, and niche — not cross-platform averages.

### Twitter/X

**Overall engagement rate (2025-2026):** 0.12-0.15%

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Avg. engagement rate | 0.12% (down from 0.15% in 2024) |
| Good engagement rate | 0.5-1.0% |
| Excellent engagement rate | 1.0%+ |
| Posting frequency (recommended) | 3-5x per day for growth, 1-2x for maintenance |

**Engagement by content format (median):**
| Format | Engagement Rate |
|--------|----------------|
| Text posts | 3.56% |
| Image posts | 3.40% |
| Video posts | 2.96% |
| Link posts | 2.25% |

**Notes:**
- X engagement has declined consistently since 2023 platform changes
- Text-first posts outperform media-heavy posts (reversal from prior years)
- Threads and long-form text are seeing renewed engagement
- Reply-driven engagement (conversation starters) outperforms broadcast-style posts

**Niche benchmarks:**
- Tech/SaaS: 0.3-0.8%
- Personal brand / thought leadership: 0.5-2.0%
- Media/news: 0.1-0.3%
- Creator/entertainment: 0.4-1.5%

### Instagram

**Overall engagement rate (2025-2026):** 0.48-0.70%

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Avg. engagement rate (all accounts) | 0.48% |
| Good engagement rate | 1.5-3.0% |
| Excellent engagement rate | 3.0%+ |
| Posting frequency (recommended) | 4-7x per week (feed + Reels combined) |

**Engagement by follower count:**
| Follower Tier | Avg. Engagement Rate |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 1K-5K (nano) | 3.0-5.0% |
| 5K-20K (micro) | 1.8-3.5% |
| 20K-100K (mid) | 1.2-2.5% |
| 100K-500K (macro) | 0.8-1.8% |
| 500K+ (mega) | 0.5-1.2% |

**Engagement by content format:**
| Format | Avg. Engagement |
|--------|----------------|
| Reels | Highest reach, moderate engagement |
| Carousels | Highest engagement rate, strong saves |
| Single image | Moderate, declining |
| Stories | High for existing audience, low reach |

**Healthy metric ratios:**
- Save rate: 2-5% of total engagement indicates high-value content
- Share rate: 1-3% indicates broad appeal
- Comment-to-like ratio: >2% indicates strong community
- Reels completion rate: 38% average (aim for 50%+)

**Niche benchmarks:**
- Education/how-to: 2.0-4.2%
- Fitness/health: 1.5-3.0%
- Business/finance: 1.2-3.8%
- Lifestyle/travel: 1.5-3.5%
- Food/dining: 2.0-3.5%

### YouTube

**Overall engagement rate (2025-2026):** ~2.0%

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Avg. engagement rate | 2.0% |
| Good engagement rate | 3-7% |
| Excellent engagement rate | 7%+ (exceptional: 10%+) |
| Posting frequency (recommended) | 1-2x per week (long-form), 3-5x per week (Shorts) |

**Engagement by content type:**
| Format | Avg. Engagement Rate |
|--------|---------------------|
| Long-form video | 2-4% |
| YouTube Shorts | 2-6% (higher ratio per view) |

**Key performance metrics:**
| Metric | Good Benchmark |
|--------|---------------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 4-10% (thumbnails + titles) |
| Average view duration | 50%+ of video length |
| Like-to-view ratio | 4-5% |
| Comment-to-view ratio | 0.5-1.0% |
| Subscriber conversion rate | 1-3% of viewers per video |

**Niche benchmarks (comment-to-view ratio):**
- Gaming: 0.078%
- Education: 0.5-1.5%
- Business/entrepreneurship: 0.3-0.8%
- Music: 0.018%
- Tech reviews: 0.3-1.0%

**Notes:**
- Long-form views are up 121% vs. six months ago (Q4 2025 data)
- Shorts views are down 20% vs. six months ago
- Audience retention curve matters more than total views
- Community tab engagement is an underused growth lever

**Overall engagement rate (2025-2026):** 3.70-4.86%

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Avg. engagement rate | 3.70% (up 49% YoY) |
| Good engagement rate | 5-8% |
| Excellent engagement rate | 8%+ |
| Posting frequency (recommended) | 5-7x per week (minimum 3x) |

**Engagement by follower count:**
| Follower Tier | Avg. Engagement Rate |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 1K-10K (nano/micro) | 5.0-7.4% |
| 10K-50K (mid) | 3.5-5.5% |

| 500K+ (mega) | 0.85-3.0% |

**Key performance metrics:**
| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Video completion rate | 52% average (aim for 60%+) |
| Share rate | Up 45% YoY — shares are the key growth signal |
| Save rate | High saves indicate evergreen/tutorial value |

**TikTok vs. Instagram Reels:**
| Metric | TikTok | Instagram Reels |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Avg. engagement rate | 3.70-4.86% | 0.48-1.5% |

**Notes:**
- TikTok engagement is the highest of any major platform
- Comments per post fell 24% YoY, but shares surged 45% — passive engagement is shifting to distribution actions
- The algorithm rewards watch time and rewatches above all else
- Niche communities (BookTok, FinTok, etc.) have significantly higher engagement than general content

**Niche benchmarks:**
- Education/how-to: 4.0-7.0%
- Entertainment/comedy: 5.0-8.0%
- Business/finance (FinTok): 3.0-5.0%
- Health/fitness: 3.5-6.0%
- Tech: 2.5-4.5%

## Part 3: Cross-Platform Audit Principles

### Engagement Rate Formulas

Each platform calculates engagement differently. Use the correct formula:

- **Twitter/X:** (Likes + Replies + Retweets + Bookmarks) / Impressions x 100
- **Instagram:** (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Reach x 100 (or / Followers for comparability)
- **YouTube:** (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Views x 100
- **LinkedIn:** (Likes + Comments + Shares + Clicks) / Impressions x 100
- **TikTok:** (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Views x 100

### Context Matters

When applying benchmarks, always account for:

1. **Follower count tier** — Smaller accounts naturally have higher engagement rates
2. **Niche/industry** — B2B and finance typically have lower rates than lifestyle and entertainment
3. **Content format** — Compare carousel to carousel, not carousel to text post
4. **Posting frequency** — High-frequency posters often have lower per-post engagement but higher total engagement
5. **Account age** — New accounts often have inflated rates from early-follower enthusiasm
6. **Paid vs. organic** — Separate paid reach from organic when calculating true engagement
7. **Platform trends** — A declining platform average means a flat account rate is actually gaining ground relatively

### Red Flags to Surface

During any audit, flag these immediately:

- Engagement rate below 50% of platform benchmark for follower tier
- Follower growth with declining engagement (likely bot/fake followers)
- High impressions but near-zero saves/shares (low content value)
- Comment section dominated by spam, bots, or pod groups
- Sudden spikes followed by drops (giveaway-driven growth or viral one-offs without retention)
- No content in 30+ days (account dormancy)
- Inconsistent branding across platform profiles

### Audit Output Structure

Every platform audit should produce:

1. **Scorecard** — All six dimensions scored 1-10 with evidence
2. **Benchmark comparison** — Account metrics vs. platform benchmarks for their tier
3. **Competitor snapshot** — Side-by-side with 3-5 competitors
4. **Top 3 strengths** — What is working and should be amplified
5. **Top 3 improvement areas** — What needs immediate attention
6. **Actionable recommendations** — Prioritized list with expected impact (high/medium/low)
7. **90-day action plan** — Specific next steps tied to the recommendations
