## 1. Calorie Estimation — TDEE

### What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories burned per
day, accounting for all activity. It is the foundation of any nutrition plan.

### TDEE Components

| Component | % of TDEE | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **BMR** (Basal Metabolic Rate) | 60-70% | Calories burned at complete rest to sustain life functions |
| **TEF** (Thermic Effect of Food) | 8-15% | Energy used to digest, absorb, and process food |
| **NEAT** (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) | 15-30% | All non-exercise movement (walking, fidgeting, standing) |
| **EAT** (Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) | 5-10% | Intentional exercise |

### BMR Calculation — Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

The most accurate widely-used equation:

```
Men:    BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age) + 5
Women:  BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age) - 161
```

### Activity Multipliers

Multiply BMR by the appropriate factor to get TDEE:

| Activity Level | Multiplier | Description |
|---------------|------------|-------------|
| Sedentary | 1.2 | Desk job, little to no exercise |
| Lightly active | 1.375 | Light exercise 1-3 days/week |
| Moderately active | 1.55 | Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week |
| Very active | 1.725 | Hard exercise 6-7 days/week |
| Extremely active | 1.9 | Very hard exercise, physical job, 2x/day training |

### Setting Calorie Targets by Goal

| Goal | Calorie Target | Rate of Change |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Fat loss (conservative)** | TDEE - 250 kcal/day | ~0.25 kg/week loss |
| **Fat loss (moderate)** | TDEE - 500 kcal/day | ~0.5 kg/week loss |
| **Fat loss (aggressive)** | TDEE - 750 kcal/day | ~0.75 kg/week loss |
| **Maintenance** | TDEE | Weight stable |
| **Lean bulk (conservative)** | TDEE + 200-300 kcal/day | ~0.25 kg/week gain |
| **Lean bulk (moderate)** | TDEE + 300-500 kcal/day | ~0.35 kg/week gain |
| **Aggressive bulk** | TDEE + 500-750 kcal/day | ~0.5 kg/week gain |

### Important Caveats

- TDEE calculators provide **estimates** — actual needs vary by 10-15%
- Start with the calculated target, then **adjust based on real-world results**
- Weigh daily, take weekly averages, and adjust every 2-3 weeks
- Weight fluctuates 1-3 kg daily due to water, food volume, sodium, and other factors
- If losing weight too fast (>1% bodyweight per week), increase calories
- If not losing weight after 2-3 weeks at calculated deficit, reduce by another 100-200 kcal

## 2. Macronutrient Fundamentals

### The Three Macros

| Macro | Calories per gram | Primary Function |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Protein** | 4 kcal/g | Muscle repair/growth, enzymes, hormones, satiety |
| **Carbohydrates** | 4 kcal/g | Primary energy source, brain fuel, exercise performance |
| **Fat** | 9 kcal/g | Hormone production, cell membrane integrity, fat-soluble vitamin absorption |

### Protein

**Why protein is the priority macro:**
- Highest thermic effect of food (20-30% of calories burned during digestion)
- Most satiating macro (helps control hunger)
- Essential for muscle preservation during fat loss
- Essential for muscle building during surplus
- Cannot be stored efficiently — must be consumed regularly

**Daily protein targets:**

| Goal | Protein Target | Reasoning |
|------|---------------|-----------|
| General health | 0.8 g/kg bodyweight | Minimum RDA for sedentary adults |
| Active individual | 1.2-1.6 g/kg | Supports activity demands |
| Fat loss | 1.6-2.4 g/kg | Higher protein preserves muscle in deficit |
| Muscle building | 1.6-2.2 g/kg | Optimal range for muscle protein synthesis |
| Lean individual cutting | 2.2-2.6 g/kg | Higher end needed when already lean |

**High-quality protein sources:**

| Source | Protein per 100g | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Chicken breast | 31g | Lean, versatile |
| Turkey breast | 29g | Lean alternative to chicken |
| Lean beef (93/7) | 26g | Rich in iron, creatine, B12 |
| Salmon | 25g | Omega-3 fatty acids |
| Tuna (canned) | 26g | Convenient, affordable |
| Eggs (whole) | 13g | Complete protein, healthy fats |
| Greek yogurt (nonfat) | 10g | Casein protein, gut health |
| Cottage cheese | 11g | Slow-digesting casein |
| Tofu (firm) | 17g | Plant-based complete protein |
| Lentils (cooked) | 9g | Plant-based, high fiber |
| Whey protein powder | 75-80g | Fast-digesting, convenient |

### Carbohydrates

**Functions:**
- Primary fuel for high-intensity exercise
- Brain's preferred energy source (~120g/day minimum)
- Spares protein from being used as fuel
- Supports mood and cognitive function
- Fiber (a carbohydrate) supports digestive health

**Carb quality spectrum:**

| Category | Examples | When to Use |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Complex/slow | Oats, sweet potato, brown rice, quinoa | Base of most meals |
| Fibrous | Vegetables, leafy greens, broccoli | Every meal — volume and micronutrients |
| Fruit | Berries, banana, apple, oranges | Snacks, pre/post workout |
| Simple/fast | White rice, white bread, sports drinks | Post-workout, performance fueling |
| Sugary | Candy, soda, pastries | Minimize — low nutritional value |

**Fiber targets:** 25-35g per day (from vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes)

### Fat

**Functions:**
- Essential for testosterone and estrogen production
- Required for absorption of vitamins A, D, E, K
- Cell membrane structure
- Brain health (brain is ~60% fat)
- Provides sustained energy for low-intensity activity

**Minimum fat intake:** Do not go below 0.5 g/kg bodyweight — hormonal function
suffers below this threshold.

**Fat quality:**

| Type | Sources | Recommendation |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Monounsaturated | Olive oil, avocado, nuts, almonds | Prioritize — heart health benefits |
| Polyunsaturated (omega-3) | Fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts, chia seeds | Prioritize — anti-inflammatory |
| Polyunsaturated (omega-6) | Vegetable oils, seeds | Moderate — already abundant in most diets |
| Saturated | Butter, coconut oil, red meat, cheese | Moderate — keep under 10% of total calories |
| Trans fat | Fried food, processed snacks, margarine | Avoid — no health benefits |

## 3. Macros by Goal

### Fat Loss

**Priority order:** Protein > Fat (minimum) > Carbs/Fat (fill remaining)

**Step-by-step macro calculation:**

1. Set calories: TDEE - 500 kcal
2. Set protein: 2.0 g/kg bodyweight
3. Set fat: 0.8-1.0 g/kg bodyweight
4. Set carbs: Remaining calories / 4

**Example for an 80kg person, TDEE 2500:**

```
Calories:  2000 kcal (TDEE - 500)
Protein:   160g (80 x 2.0) = 640 kcal
Fat:       70g  (80 x 0.875) = 630 kcal
Carbs:     (2000 - 640 - 630) / 4 = 183g = 730 kcal
```

**Macro split: ~32% protein / 32% fat / 36% carbs**

**Key principles during fat loss:**
- Keep protein high to preserve muscle
- Do not cut fat below 0.5 g/kg (hormone health)
- Reduce carbs first when adjusting calories down
- Prioritize fiber-rich, voluminous foods for satiety
- Avoid drinking calories (except protein shakes if needed)

### Muscle Gain (Lean Bulk)

**Priority order:** Protein > Carbs > Fat

**Step-by-step macro calculation:**

1. Set calories: TDEE + 300 kcal
2. Set protein: 1.8 g/kg bodyweight
3. Set fat: 0.8-1.0 g/kg bodyweight
4. Set carbs: Remaining calories / 4

**Example for an 80kg person, TDEE 2500:**

```
Calories:  2800 kcal (TDEE + 300)
Protein:   145g (80 x 1.8) = 580 kcal
Fat:       75g  (80 x 0.94) = 675 kcal
Carbs:     (2800 - 580 - 675) / 4 = 386g = 1545 kcal
```

**Macro split: ~21% protein / 24% fat / 55% carbs**

**Key principles during muscle gain:**
- Carbs fuel training performance and recovery
- Surplus should be modest to minimize fat gain
- Higher carbs around training (pre/post workout)
- Track weight gain rate — aim for 0.25-0.5 kg/week

### Maintenance

**Priority order:** Protein > balanced Carbs/Fat

**Step-by-step macro calculation:**

1. Set calories: TDEE
2. Set protein: 1.6 g/kg bodyweight
3. Set fat: 0.8-1.0 g/kg bodyweight
4. Set carbs: Remaining calories / 4

**Example for an 80kg person, TDEE 2500:**

```
Calories:  2500 kcal
Protein:   130g (80 x 1.6) = 520 kcal
Fat:       75g  (80 x 0.94) = 675 kcal
Carbs:     (2500 - 520 - 675) / 4 = 326g = 1305 kcal
```

**Macro split: ~21% protein / 27% fat / 52% carbs**

### Quick Macro Reference Table

| Goal | Protein (g/kg) | Fat (g/kg) | Carbs | Calorie Adjustment |
|------|---------------|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Fat loss | 2.0-2.4 | 0.7-1.0 | Remaining | TDEE - 300 to -750 |
| Lean bulk | 1.6-2.0 | 0.8-1.0 | Remaining (high) | TDEE + 200 to +500 |
| Maintenance | 1.4-1.8 | 0.8-1.0 | Remaining | TDEE |
| Recomp | 2.0-2.2 | 0.8-1.0 | Remaining | TDEE (or slight deficit) |

## 4. Meal Timing and Nutrient Timing

### How Many Meals Per Day?

**The research says:**
- Total daily intake matters more than meal frequency
- 3-5 meals per day is practical for most people
- For muscle building: 3-4 protein feedings spaced 3-5 hours apart is optimal
  for maximizing muscle protein synthesis
- For fat loss: Meal frequency should support adherence and satiety

**Practical recommendation:** 3 main meals + 1-2 snacks, depending on schedule
and calorie target.

### Protein Distribution

Spreading protein across meals matters more than total timing:

| Approach | Description | Effectiveness |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Even distribution | ~30-40g protein per meal, 4 meals | Optimal for muscle protein synthesis |
| Skewed (e.g., big dinner) | Most protein in 1-2 meals | Suboptimal but still effective if total is met |
| Protein pacing | 0.4 g/kg per meal, 4+ meals | Research-supported for muscle building |

**Minimum per meal for MPS (muscle protein synthesis):** ~20-40g protein per
feeding, depending on body size and protein source.

### Pre-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** 1-3 hours before training

| Macro | Target | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Protein | 20-40g | Amino acids available during training |
| Carbs | 30-60g | Fuel for high-intensity work |
| Fat | Low-moderate | Slows digestion, keep low if eating close to training |

**Example pre-workout meals:**
- Chicken breast + rice (2-3 hours before)
- Greek yogurt + banana + granola (1-2 hours before)
- Protein shake + oats (1 hour before)
- Toast + peanut butter + banana (1 hour before)

### Post-Workout Nutrition

**Timing:** Within 1-2 hours after training (the "anabolic window" is wider
than the old 30-minute myth suggests — especially if you ate pre-workout)

| Macro | Target | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Protein | 30-50g | Kickstart muscle repair |
| Carbs | 40-80g | Replenish glycogen, spike insulin for nutrient uptake |
| Fat | Low | Does not impair recovery, but slows absorption |

**Key insight from research:** If you ate a mixed meal 2-3 hours before training,
there is less urgency for immediate post-workout nutrition. The amino acids from
the pre-workout meal are still available. If you trained fasted, post-workout
protein becomes more important.

### Evening and Before-Bed Nutrition

- A casein protein source (cottage cheese, casein shake) before bed can support
  overnight muscle protein synthesis
- Avoid large, heavy meals within 1-2 hours of sleep (sleep quality impact)
- A light protein-rich snack is fine

## 5. Dietary Guidelines and Food Quality

### The 80/20 Principle

- **80% of intake:** Whole, minimally processed, nutrient-dense foods
- **20% of intake:** Flexible choices for enjoyment and adherence

This approach is more sustainable than 100% "clean eating" and produces
equivalent body composition results when macros and calories are matched.

### Food Quality Priorities

| Priority | Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Lean proteins | Chicken, turkey, fish, eggs, lean beef, tofu, legumes |
| 2 | Vegetables | Leafy greens, broccoli, peppers, onions, tomatoes |
| 3 | Complex carbs | Oats, rice, sweet potato, quinoa, whole grain bread |
| 4 | Fruits | Berries, bananas, apples, oranges, kiwi |
| 5 | Healthy fats | Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish |
| 6 | Dairy | Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, milk, cheese |

### Micronutrient Considerations

Key micronutrients that active individuals should monitor:

| Nutrient | Daily Target | Top Sources |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Vitamin D | 1000-2000 IU | Sunlight, fatty fish, fortified foods |
| Iron | 8-18 mg | Red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals |
| Calcium | 1000-1200 mg | Dairy, fortified plant milk, leafy greens |
| Magnesium | 310-420 mg | Nuts, seeds, dark chocolate, spinach |
| Zinc | 8-11 mg | Meat, shellfish, legumes, seeds |
| Omega-3 | 1-3g EPA+DHA | Fatty fish (2-3 servings/week), fish oil |
| Potassium | 2600-3400 mg | Banana, potato, avocado, beans |

### Foods to Emphasize vs Minimize

**Emphasize:**
- High-protein foods at every meal
- Colorful vegetables (aim for 5+ servings/day)
- Whole grains over refined grains
- Water as primary beverage
- Fibrous foods for satiety and gut health

**Minimize:**
- Ultra-processed foods (high calorie density, low satiety)
- Added sugars (limit to <10% of total calories)
- Alcohol (empty calories, impairs recovery, disrupts sleep)
- Excessive sodium (>2300 mg/day, unless sweating heavily)
- Trans fats (avoid entirely)

## 6. Meal Structure and Templates

### Daily Meal Template

```
BREAKFAST (25-30% of daily calories)
- Protein source: eggs, Greek yogurt, protein shake
- Complex carb: oats, whole grain toast, fruit
- Fat: nuts, seeds, avocado, natural nut butter
- Vegetable/fruit: berries, banana, spinach (in smoothie)

LUNCH (30-35% of daily calories)
- Protein source: chicken, turkey, fish, tofu, legumes
- Complex carb: rice, quinoa, sweet potato, whole grain bread
- Vegetables: large portion, multiple colors
- Fat: olive oil dressing, avocado, nuts

SNACK (10-15% of daily calories)
- Protein-focused: Greek yogurt, protein bar, cottage cheese, jerky
- Carb/fat pairing: fruit + nuts, rice cakes + nut butter

DINNER (25-35% of daily calories)
- Protein source: lean meat, fish, plant protein
- Complex carb: rice, potato, pasta (portion controlled)
- Vegetables: large portion, roasted or steamed
- Fat: cooking oil, cheese, avocado
```

## 7. Meal Prep Strategies

### The Batch Cooking Method

**When:** 1-2 prep sessions per week (Sunday + optional Wednesday)

**Core approach:**
1. Cook 3-4 protein sources in bulk
2. Prepare 2-3 carb sources in bulk
3. Wash and chop vegetables
4. Portion into individual containers
5. Refrigerate (3-4 days) or freeze (1-2 weeks)

### Prep Day Workflow

```
PREP SESSION (60-90 minutes):

1. START SLOW COOKERS/OVENS FIRST (10 min)
   - Put chicken breasts in oven (200C/400F, 25 min)
   - Start rice cooker
   - Put sweet potatoes in oven

2. WHILE PROTEINS COOK (20 min)
   - Wash and chop all vegetables
   - Prepare salad bases in containers
   - Hard boil eggs
   - Make overnight oats for 3-4 days

3. WHILE CARBS FINISH (15 min)
   - Prepare sauces and dressings
   - Portion snacks into bags (nuts, fruit)
   - Cook ground turkey/beef in skillet

4. ASSEMBLE AND PORTION (20 min)
   - Weigh and portion proteins into containers
   - Add carb portions
   - Add vegetables
   - Label with contents and date

5. STORE
   - Fridge: meals for next 3-4 days
   - Freezer: remaining meals (if >4 days out)
```

### Batch-Friendly Foods

| Category | Best for Batch Prep | Shelf Life (fridge) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Protein | Chicken breast, ground turkey, hard-boiled eggs, pulled chicken/pork | 3-4 days |
| Carbs | Rice, quinoa, sweet potato, pasta, oats | 4-5 days |
| Vegetables | Roasted broccoli/peppers/zucchini, raw chopped (bell peppers, carrots, cucumber) | 3-5 days |
| Sauces | Marinara, stir-fry sauce, vinaigrettes | 5-7 days |
| Snacks | Energy balls, portioned nuts, cut fruit | 3-5 days |

### Containers and Portioning

- Use a food scale for accuracy (especially during fat loss)
- Glass containers for reheating (no plastic + microwave)
- Label containers with protein/carb/fat grams if tracking
- Separate wet ingredients (sauces) from dry to maintain freshness

### Meal Prep Tips for Sustainability

1. **Cook proteins plainly, vary sauces** — same chicken, different flavor each day
2. **Prep components, not full meals** — mix and match at mealtime for variety
3. **Keep 2-3 "emergency meals"** — frozen meals or simple options for days you cannot prep
4. **Rotate recipes every 2-3 weeks** — prevents boredom
5. **Track what you actually eat vs plan** — adjust next week accordingly

## 8. Dietary Accommodations

### Common Dietary Patterns

| Diet | Key Rules | Macro Impact |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Standard** | No restrictions | Full flexibility |
| **Vegetarian** | No meat/fish | Protein from eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu |
| **Vegan** | No animal products | Protein from legumes, tofu, tempeh, seitan; B12 supplementation |
| **Pescatarian** | No meat, fish allowed | Fish + vegetarian protein sources |
| **Keto** | Very low carb (<50g/day) | High fat, moderate protein, very low carb |
| **Paleo** | No processed foods, grains, dairy | Moderate to high protein, moderate fat and carb |
| **Mediterranean** | Emphasis on olive oil, fish, vegetables, whole grains | Balanced macros, high healthy fat |
| **Halal/Kosher** | Religious dietary laws | Standard macros, specific food sourcing |
| **Gluten-free** | No wheat/barley/rye | Swap grains for rice, potato, GF alternatives |

### Vegetarian/Vegan Protein Strategies

Meeting protein targets without meat requires intentional planning:

| Food | Protein/100g | Complete Protein? |
|------|-------------|-------------------|

| Tempeh | 20g | Yes |
| Seitan | 25g | No (low lysine) |
| Lentils (cooked) | 9g | No (low methionine) |
| Chickpeas (cooked) | 9g | No (low methionine) |

| Edamame | 11g | Yes |
| Quinoa (cooked) | 4g | Yes |
| Nutritional yeast | 50g | Yes |

**Combining for complete proteins:** Legumes + grains (rice and beans, hummus

### Common Allergies and Substitutions

| Gluten | Rice, potato, corn, quinoa, GF oats |
| Soy | Other plant proteins (pea, hemp), meat-based alternatives |
| Shellfish | Other fish, poultry, plant protein |

## 9. Hydration

### Daily Water Targets

| Baseline | Additional |
|----------|-----------|
| ~35 ml per kg bodyweight | +500-1000 ml per hour of exercise |
| Example: 80kg = 2.8 liters/day | +additional in hot/humid conditions |

### Hydration Markers

| Indicator | Well Hydrated | Dehydrated |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Urine color | Pale yellow (straw) | Dark yellow/amber |
| Urine frequency | Every 2-4 hours | Infrequent |
| Thirst | Minimal | Persistent |
| Energy | Normal | Fatigue, headache |
| Performance | Normal | Decreased strength/endurance |

### Hydration Tips

- Start the day with 500 ml water upon waking
- Carry a water bottle throughout the day
- Drink before, during, and after exercise
- Caffeinated beverages count toward hydration (mild diuretic effect is offset)
- Fruits and vegetables contribute to hydration (~20% of daily intake)

## 10. Supplements — Evidence-Based Basics

Only supplements with strong research support. Most needs should be met through
whole foods first.

| Supplement | Evidence Level | Recommended For | Typical Dose |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Creatine monohydrate** | Very strong | Strength, muscle, cognitive | 3-5g daily |
| **Whey/plant protein** | Strong | Meeting protein targets | As needed to hit targets |
| **Vitamin D** | Strong | If limited sun exposure | 1000-2000 IU daily |
| **Omega-3 (fish oil)** | Strong | If not eating fatty fish 2-3x/week | 1-3g EPA+DHA daily |
| **Magnesium** | Moderate | Sleep, recovery, if intake is low | 200-400 mg daily |
| **Caffeine** | Strong | Pre-workout performance | 3-6 mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-training |
| **Multivitamin** | Low-moderate | Insurance during caloric deficit | 1 daily (during deficit only) |

**Important:** Supplements are not a substitute for a well-structured diet. Fix
food quality and quantity first.

## 11. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Not tracking intake | Cannot manage what you do not measure | Track at least during learning phase (2-4 weeks) |
| Underestimating calories | Eating more than you think | Weigh food with a scale, especially calorie-dense foods |
| Insufficient protein | Muscle loss during deficit, poor recovery | Prioritize protein first in every meal |
| Cutting fat too low | Hormonal disruption | Never go below 0.5g/kg bodyweight |
| Eliminating food groups | Nutrient deficiencies, unsustainable | Include all macros unless medical reason |
| Drinking calories | Easy to overconsume, low satiety | Eat whole foods, limit liquid calories |
| Weekend overeating | Erases weekday deficit | Plan social meals, track weekend eating too |
| All-or-nothing mindset | One "bad" meal leads to "bad" day/week | One meal does not ruin progress; resume plan |
| Overcomplicating meals | Leads to giving up on prep | Keep meals simple with 3-4 ingredients |
| Ignoring vegetables | Missing fiber, micronutrients, volume | Add vegetables to every lunch and dinner |
| Not adjusting over time | Body adapts, needs change | Reassess every 2-4 weeks based on results |
| Relying on supplements | False sense of nutritional security | Whole food first, supplements fill gaps only |

## 12. Quick-Reference Tables and Checklists

### Macro Calculation Cheat Sheet

```
1. Calculate BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor):
   Men:   (10 x kg) + (6.25 x cm) - (5 x age) + 5
   Women: (10 x kg) + (6.25 x cm) - (5 x age) - 161

2. Multiply by activity factor to get TDEE:
   Sedentary: 1.2 | Light: 1.375 | Moderate: 1.55 | Active: 1.725

3. Adjust for goal:
   Fat loss: TDEE - 500 | Maintain: TDEE | Bulk: TDEE + 300

4. Set protein: 1.6-2.4 g/kg (higher for fat loss)
5. Set fat: 0.7-1.0 g/kg
6. Set carbs: (remaining calories) / 4
```

## Key Principles — Summary

1. **Calories determine weight change.** Surplus = gain. Deficit = lose. No exceptions.
2. **Protein is the priority macro.** Set it first, always.
3. **Do not fear fat.** It is essential for hormones and health. Just do not overeat it.
4. **Carbs are fuel, not the enemy.** Especially for active individuals.
5. **Meal timing matters less than total intake.** But protein distribution across meals helps.
6. **Consistency beats perfection.** An 80% adherent plan beats a 100% plan followed for 3 days.
7. **Track, measure, adjust.** Use data, not feelings, to guide changes.
8. **Keep it simple.** Complex plans get abandoned. Simple, repeatable meals win.
9. **Meal prep is a force multiplier.** 90 minutes on Sunday saves hours during the week.
10. **Reassess regularly.** Bodies adapt. Adjust every 2-4 weeks based on results.
