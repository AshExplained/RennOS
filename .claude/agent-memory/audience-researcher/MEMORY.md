## 2026-03-22 — Initial Persona Build for ashexplained

### Task
Built 4 primary audience personas from scratch based on Brand DNA + competitive context.

### Personas Created
1. **The Overwhelmed Professional** — marketing/ops/HR/PM, 30-42, has heard of AI but has not meaningfully adopted it, embarrassed by the gap, needs a pressure-free entry point
2. **The Efficiency-Seeking Freelancer** — copywriter/designer/consultant, 27-40, already uses AI ad-hoc, needs structured workflows and honest tool comparisons, ROI-focused
3. **The Curious but Skeptical Professional** — senior IC or manager, 33-48, high information literacy, distrusts hype from all directions, wants depth and honest methodology, most likely to share quality content in professional networks
4. **The Creator Going Through an AI Identity Crisis** — content creator/writer, 25-37, craft pride creates AI anxiety, wants to use AI "on their own terms" without losing voice or identity

### Key Cross-Persona Patterns (Important for content strategy)
- Trust is the primary currency — all four are hype-skeptical and looking for a reliable source
- "What can I do with this?" is the universal first filter — practicality hooks before depth pays off
- None want to become "AI people" — framing of "AI to serve your existing work" outperforms "go all-in on AI"
- Anxiety is real but understated — never name it directly, address it through calm and practical framing
- Shareable content = "I finally understand this" moment — design for that feeling

### Secondary Audiences (Not Fully Profiled Yet)
- Developer Staying Current: mid-senior eng, needs signal on AI landscape for their tools/career, not building AI
- Student/Career Switcher: 20-26, high reach but low purchasing power, useful for top-of-funnel only

### Competitive Gap Identified
- Alex Finn: tutorial-focused, builder-oriented audience — ashexplained goes deeper on "why"
- Greg Isenberg: business-opportunity framing — ashexplained is education-first, not "build an AI business"
- Nick Saraev: automation agency audience — ashexplained serves existing work, not agency-building
- Matt Pocock: developer-specific — ashexplained serves the broader non-technical professional
- Gap ashexplained can own: "use AI on your own terms" positioning for Persona 4 (Creators) — nobody holds this explicitly
